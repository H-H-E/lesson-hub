# Grade 1 Science: All About Matter
## A Systems Literacy Journey into the Stuff That Makes Up Our World

---

## 1. SMART Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

1. **Identify and describe** the three states of matter (solid, liquid, gas) using their own words and at least two properties of each, with 80% accuracy. *(Specific, Measurable)*

2. **Observe and compare** how matter changes from one state to another when heated or cooled, documenting changes in their Explorer's Journal with drawings and labels. *(Specific, Measurable, Achievable)*

3. **Explain** how they are part of a larger system—made of matter that comes from the Earth, cycles through nature, and connects to all living things—using the phrase "I am part of Earth's matter system." *(Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)*

4. **Collaborate** with a partner to sort objects from the classroom into the three states of matter and justify their reasoning using observational evidence. *(Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)*

## 3. Engaging Hook: "The Mystery Matter Box"

**Timing: 5 minutes**

**Teacher sets up:**
Before students enter, place a sealed cardboard box on a table with three holes cut in the top (just big enough for a child to fit their hand through). Inside the box, place: an ice cube, a wet sponge, and a balloon (deflated).

**Gather students on the carpet and say:**

"Welcome, Explorers! Today we're going on a matter adventure. But first, I need your super-detective powers."

Hold up the Mystery Box.

"This box has three secrets inside. I can't open it, but you can use your hands to explore. Here's the rule: you must describe what you feel WITHOUT saying what you think it is. Let's see if we can figure out what matter is doing inside!"

**Invite 3 volunteers:**

1. **First volunteer** reaches in, touches the ice cube: "It's really cold! It's hard but smooth. It makes my fingers really cold!"

2. **Second volunteer** reaches in, touches the wet sponge: "It's soft and squishy! It's wet. It feels like it's holding something wet inside."

3. **Third volunteer** reaches in, touches the balloon: "It's light and stretchy! There's nothing inside—or is there air? It's bouncy!"

**After each, chart their observations on the board in two columns:**

| What it feels like | What it might be doing |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Cold, hard, smooth | Solid |
| Soft, squishy, wet | Liquid |
| Light, stretchy, bouncy | Gas (air) |

**Connect to the lesson:**

"Great detective work! What you discovered is that matter can be in different forms. Some matter is solid (like the ice), some is liquid (like water), and some is gas (like air). Today, we're going to become Matter Explorers and learn how we are part of something MUCH bigger—a system that connects EVERYTHING!"

## 4. Complete Lesson Sequence with Timing

### Total Lesson Time: 60 minutes

---

### Part 1: Hook & Introduction (10 minutes)

- **4 min:** Mystery Matter Box activity (see Section 3)
- **3 min:** Introduce the three states of matter using hand motions:
  - *Solid* = stomp feet, make fists (matter holds its shape)
  - *Liquid* = wiggle fingers, pour invisible water (matter flows and takes shape of container)
  - *Gas* = wave arms like wind, puff cheeks and blow (matter spreads out everywhere)
- **3 min:** Introduce the Systems Literacy big idea: "Today we'll discover that you are made of matter, and that matter connects you to EVERYTHING on Earth!"

---

### Part 2: Guided Exploration – "Matter Walk" (15 minutes)

**Timing: 15 minutes**

**Setup:** Students walk around the classroom in pairs with their "Matter Detective" cards.

**Instructions:**
"Walk slowly around our classroom. When you find something made of matter, stop and talk to your partner about: What is it? Is it a solid, liquid, or gas? How do you know?"

**Guided Questions (circulate and prompt):**
- "What matter is making up that chair? Is it solid?"
- "Look at the water fountain—is that water solid or liquid? How can you tell?"
- "Can you see air? Where might it be hiding? What makes you think there's air?"

**Gather back:** Each pair shares one discovery. Create a class list on chart paper.

**Systems Connection (2 min):**
"Every single thing you found is made of matter—even the air! And here's the amazing part: ALL of this matter has been on Earth for billions of years. The atoms in your body have been in stars, in dinosaurs, in rain, in trees. You are part of Earth's matter system!"

---

### Part 3: Hands-On Exploration – "Matter Station Rotation" (20 minutes)

**Timing: 20 minutes**

**Set up 4 stations, students rotate every 5 minutes:**

| Station | Activity | Materials |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **Station 1: Solid Sort** | Sort objects into solids; notice they hold shape | Rocks, pencils, blocks, books, cotton ball |
| **Station 2: Liquid Pour** | Pour water between cups; observe how it takes container's shape | Clear cups, water, food coloring |
| **Station 3: Gas Hunt** | Find evidence of air/gas (balloons, bubbles, fan, blow on hand) | Balloons, straw in water, hand fan |
| **Station 4: Matter Change** | Hold ice cube, watch it melt; discuss heat = change | Ice cups, warm hands, timers |

**Teacher circulates**, prompting:
- "What do you notice?"
- "How is this different from the other states?"
- "What do you think will happen next?"

---

### Part 4: Systems Literacy Connection – "You Are Matter!" (5 minutes)

**Timing: 5 minutes**

**Gather students. Read aloud slowly:**

*"You are not a lone creature being bossed around by morality; you are a living process inside other living processes."*

—Adapted for Grade 1 understanding

**Explain in child-friendly language:**

"This means you are NOT separate from the world. You are a living part of Earth's big living system. The water in your body might have been in a river, or in a cloud, or in a tree yesterday! The air you breathe was in our classroom, outside, maybe in another country! You are a process inside bigger processes. You are matter moving through the Earth system!"

**Interactive activity:**
- "Let's breathe in together. Feel the air go into your lungs. That air was just in this room. Now breathe out. Where does that air go?"
- "Now touch your skin. That's matter too! Where do you think those atoms were before they became YOU?"

---

### Part 5: p5.js Simulation – "Matter Transformer" (5 minutes)

**Timing: 5 minutes**

**Teacher projects the simulation (see Section 14 for code).**

"Watch this! This is a computer program that shows matter changing. Watch what happens when we add HEAT to the solid (ice)... Watch it become a LIQUID (water)... Add more heat and it becomes GAS (steam)!"

**Demonstrate the simulation:**
- Click to heat/cool different matter types
- Show particles moving faster when heated
- Let 2-3 students come up to try

**Connect to real life:**
"When you have a hot bath, the water starts as a liquid but some turns into gas—that's steam! When you freeze juice, it starts as liquid and becomes a solid—ice! You're seeing matter change every day!"

---

### Part 6: Closing & Reflection (5 minutes)

**Timing: 5 minutes**

**Song: "Matter Matter Everywhere"** (to the tune of "Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes")

*Matter, matter, everywhere! (wave arms wide)*
*Solids, liquids, gas we see! (point to three fingers)*
*Matter, matter, connects us all— (point to self, then to friends)*
*We're part of Earth's great living sea! (wave like ocean)*

**Exit Ticket (1 question):**
"On a sticky note, draw one thing you learned about matter today!"

## 5. Three-Level Differentiation

### Level 1: Support (Extra Scaffolding)

**For students who need additional structure:**

- **Visual aids:** Provide a picture-based reference card showing examples of solids, liquids, and gases with photos (ice, water, air)
- **Sentence stems:** Offer word banks for journal writing: "I think this is a _____ because _____."
- **Partner support:** Pair with a confident peer for all partner work
- **Reduced options:** At stations, limit choices to 3 objects instead of 5
- **One-on-one check-ins:** Teacher checks in after each major transition
- **Modified assessment:** Accept verbal responses in addition to written

**Example modification for assessment:**
Instead of writing "solid, liquid, gas," student can point to the correct column and say the word aloud.

---

### Level 2: Core (Standard Grade-Level)

**For students meeting grade-level expectations:**

- All standard activities as described
- Challenge: Add "Explain your thinking" prompts in journal
- Opportunity to lead a station or share at the front
- Full participation in p5.js simulation (explore independently)

**Extension for fast finishers:**
- "Can you find something at this station that's made of TWO kinds of matter? What are they?"
- "Can you think of another example of this state of matter at home?"

---

### Level 3: Extension (Deeper Thinking)

**For students who need additional challenge:**

- **Research task:** "Find one thing at home that's made of matter that changes. How does it change? What causes the change?"
- **Systems thinking prompt:** "If all the water on Earth was used up, what would happen to living things? How are we connected to water?"
- **Create a game:** Design a "Matter Bingo" game with pictures of solids, liquids, and gases
- **Challenge question:** "Can matter be more than one state at once? What about clouds? Research and report!"
- **Leadership role:** Become "Matter Expert" helper at stations, explain concepts to peers
- **Simulation extension:** Use p5.js code to experiment with temperature settings and document observations

## 7. Systems Literacy Connection

### Core Concept: "You Are Part of Earth's Matter System"

**The Quote:**
> *"You are not a lone creature being bossed around by morality; you are a living process inside other living processes."*

—Adapted from complexity ethics

### How This Connects to Grade 1

This profound concept becomes accessible to Grade 1 students through these key ideas:

1. **Connection, Not Separation:** Students learn that they are NOT separate from the world around them. They are made of the same matter that makes up rocks, water, air, and all living things.

2. **Cycling Matter:** The matter in their bodies has been everywhere—rain, rivers, oceans, clouds, plants, animals, food. It cycles continuously.

3. **Living System:** The Earth is a living system where matter moves, changes, and connects all things. Humans are active participants in this system, not passive observers.

4. **Interdependence:** Just as matter connects everything, everything depends on the matter system. Clean water, air, and healthy ecosystems matter because WE ARE part of them.

### Age-Appropriate Language for Students

Instead of "You are a living process inside other living processes," we say:

- "You are made of matter that comes from the Earth!"
- "The water in your body might have been in a cloud, a river, or a tree yesterday!"
- "You are part of Earth's big matter family—connected to everything!"
- "When you breathe, you are swapping matter with the world around you!"

### Manifest in This Lesson

- **Part 4:** "You Are Matter!" reflection activity
- **Part 2:** Matter Walk connecting classroom objects to Earth system
- **Exit ticket:** Prompt to draw "one thing you learned about matter" (connecting self to content)
- **Journal prompts:** Include systems connection prompts (see Section 12)
- **Cross-curricular:** Connections to Indigenous knowledge of interconnection (Section 8)

✅ **Solids** – matter that holds its shape (rocks, pencils, ice)

## 14. p5.js Simulation Code

### "Matter Transformer" - Interactive Simulation

This p5.js sketch demonstrates matter changing states with particle animation. You can run this at: [p5.js Web Editor](https://editor.p5js.org/)

```javascript
// Matter Transformer - Grade 1 Science Lesson
// An interactive simulation showing solids, liquids, and gases

let particles = [];
let state = "solid"; // solid, liquid, gas
let temperature = 20;
let targetTemp = 20;
let matterType = "water"; // water, oxygen, iron

function setup() {
  createCanvas(600, 400);
  textSize(16);
  resetParticles();
}

function draw() {
  // Sky background gradient
  setGradient(0, 0, width, height, color(135, 206, 235), color(255, 255, 255));
  
  // Update temperature gradually
  temperature = lerp(temperature, targetTemp, 0.05);
  
  // Draw temperature gauge
  drawTemperatureGauge();
  
  // Draw matter info
  drawMatterInfo();
  
  // Draw and update particles
  for (let p of particles) {
    p.update(temperature, state);
    p.display(state);
  }
  
  // State label
  fill(0);
  noStroke();
  textSize(24);
  textAlign(CENTER);
  let stateLabel = state.charAt(0).toUpperCase() + state.slice(1);
  text("State: " + stateLabel, width/2, 40);
  
  // Draw heat/cool buttons
  drawButtons();
}

function setGradient(x, y, w, h, c1, c2) {
  noFill();
  for (let i = y; i <= y + h; i++) {
    let inter = map(i, y, y + h, 0, 1);
    let c = lerpColor(c1, c2, inter);
    stroke(c);
    line(x, i, x + w, i);
  }
}

function drawTemperatureGauge() {
  fill(255, 200);
  stroke(0);
  strokeWeight(2);
  rect(450, 50, 30, 200, 5);
  
  // Temperature bar
  let tempHeight = map(temperature, -20, 120, 180, 0);
  fill(255, 100, 100);
  noStroke();
  rect(453, 60 + tempHeight, 24, 180 - tempHeight);
  
  // Labels
  fill(0);
  noStroke();
  textSize(12);
  textAlign(RIGHT);
  text("120°", 445, 70);
  text("20°", 445, 230);
  text("Hot", 480, 70);
  text("Cold", 480, 230);
}

function drawMatterInfo() {
  fill(255, 200);
  stroke(0);
  rect(20, 50, 150, 80, 5);
  fill(0);
  noStroke();
  textSize(14);
  textAlign(LEFT);
  text("Matter: " + matterType, 30, 75);
  text("Temp: " + floor(temperature) + "°C", 30, 95);
  text("State determined by", 30, 115);
  text("temperature!", 30, 130);
}

function drawButtons() {
  // Heat button
  fill(255, 100, 100);
  stroke(0);
  rect(50, 320, 80, 40, 5);
  fill(255);
  textSize(16);

function mousePressed() {
  // Heat button
  if (mouseX > 50 && mouseX < 130 && mouseY > 320 && mouseY < 360) {
    targetTemp += 30;
  }
  // Cool button
  if (mouseX > 150 && mouseX < 230 && mouseY > 320 && mouseY < 360) {
    targetTemp -= 30;
  }
  // Matter type buttons
  if (mouseX > 300 && mouseX < 380 && mouseY > 320 && mouseY < 350) {
    matterType = "water";
    resetParticles();
  }
  if (mouseX > 390 && mouseX < 470 && mouseY > 320 && mouseY < 350) {
    matterType = "oxygen";
    resetParticles();
  }
  if (mouseX > 480 && mouseX < 560 && mouseY > 320 && mouseY < 350) {
    matterType = "iron";
    resetParticles();
  }
}

function resetParticles() {
  particles = [];
  let spacing = 25;
  for (let x = 100; x < 450; x += spacing) {
    for (let y = 80; y < 300; y += spacing) {
      particles.push(new Particle(x, y));
    }
  }
}

class Particle {
  constructor(x, y) {
    this.baseX = x;
    this.baseY = y;
    this.x = x;
    this.y = y;
    this.vx = 0;
    this.vy = 0;
  }
  
  update(temp, currentState) {
    // Determine state based on temperature
    let meltPoint = 0;
    let boilPoint = 100;
    
    if (matterType === "water") {
      meltPoint = 0;
      boilPoint = 100;
    } else if (matterType === "oxygen") {
      meltPoint = -218;
      boilPoint = -183;
    } else if (matterType === "iron") {
      meltPoint = 1538;
      boilPoint = 2862;
    }
    
    if (temp < meltPoint) {
      currentState = "solid";
    } else if (temp < boilPoint) {
      currentState = "liquid";
    } else {
      currentState = "gas";
    }
    state = currentState;
    
    // Movement based on state and temperature
    let speed = map(temp, -20, 120, 0.5, 8);
    
    if (currentState === "solid") {
      // Vibrate in place
      this.x = this.baseX + random(-2, 2);
      this.y = this.baseY + random(-2, 2);
      this.vx = 0;
      this.vy = 0;
    } else if (currentState === "liquid") {
      // Flow around
      this.vx += random(-0.5, 0.5);
      this.vy += random(-0.5, 0.5);
      this.vx *= 0.95;
      this.vy *= 0.95;
      this.x += this.vx * speed;
      this.y += this.vy * speed;
      
      // Keep in bounds
      this.x = constrain(this.x, 100, 420);
      this.y = constrain(this.y, 60, 300);
    } else {
      // Gas - move freely and fast
      this.vx += random(-1, 1);
      this.vy += random(-1, 1);
      this.vx *= 0.98;
      this.vy *= 0.98;
      this.x += this.vx * speed * 2;
      this.y += this.vy * speed * 2;
      
      // Wrap around
      if (this.x > 450) this.x = 100;
      if (this.x < 100) this.x = 450;
      if (this.y > 300) this.y = 80;
      if (this.y < 80) this.y = 300;
    }
  }
  
  display(currentState) {
    noStroke();
    
    if (currentState === "solid") {
      fill(100, 100, 200); // Blue solid
      ellipse(this.x, this.y, 20, 20);
    } else if (currentState === "liquid") {
      fill(50, 150, 255); // Blue liquid
      ellipse(this.x, this.y, 15, 15);
    } else {
      fill(200, 200, 255, 150); // Light blue gas
      ellipse(this.x, this.y, 10, 10);
    }
  }
}
```

### How to Use This Simulation in Class

1. **Projection:** Project on whiteboard for whole-class view
2. **Demonstrate:** Click "Heat" and "Cool" to show particle movement changes
3. **Explain:** Point out how particles move differently in each state
4. **Explore:** Let students come up (with assistance) to click buttons
5. **Connect:** Ask "What happens to water when we heat it?" and verify with simulation

### Activity 2: "Ice Cream in a Bag" (45 minutes)

**Description:** Make ice cream to see liquids become solids!

**Materials:**
- 1/2 cup milk
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1/2 tsp vanilla
- Ice
- 1/4 cup salt
- Small ziplock bag
- Large ziplock bag

**Steps:**
1. Mix milk, cream, sugar, vanilla in small bag, seal
2. Fill large bag halfway with ice and salt
3. Place small bag inside large bag, seal
4. Shake and roll for 5-7 minutes
5. Open and observe—liquid became solid!

**Systems connection:** "The salt helps the ice get colder faster. We're changing matter using science!"

| **Solid** | Matter that holds its shape | Rock, pencil, ice |
| **Liquid** | Matter that flows and takes container shape | Water, milk, juice |
| **Gas** | Matter that fills available space | Air, steam, helium |
| **Melt** | Change from solid to liquid | Ice → water |
| **Freeze** | Change from liquid to solid | Water → ice |
| **Evaporate** | Change from liquid to gas | Puddle → water vapor |
| **Condense** | Change from gas to liquid | Steam → water droplets |
