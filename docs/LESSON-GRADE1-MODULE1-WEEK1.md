# Grade 1 Science - Living Things
## Week 1: What is Alive?

**Duration:** 45 minutes (Day 1 of 5)

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. **Identify** living vs non-living things with 90% accuracy
2. **Describe** 3 things all living things need
3. **Explain** why some things are not alive

---

## Materials

- [ ] Interactive simulation (link in teacher guide)
- [ ] Living/Non-living picture cards (20 pairs)
- [ ] Chart paper: "Living" and "Non-Living" headers
- [ ] Crayons/markers
- [ ] Exit ticket papers

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 min)

**Teacher gathers students on carpet**

"Scientists notice things about the world. Today we're going to be scientists! Look at these pictures."

**Show:**
- Dog 🐕
- Sun ☀️
- Tree 🌳
- Car 🚗

"Raise your hand: Which of these is alive?"

**Listen for responses** (Most will say dog, maybe tree)

"Hmm. Interesting. Let's find out!"

---

### Exploration (15 min)

**Activity: Interactive Simulation**

Students go to computers/tablets OR teacher projects simulation.

**Simulation Link:** [Will be generated - p5.js simulation]

**Teacher says:**
"Watch what happens. When something is ALIVE, it does something special. Look!"

**In simulation:**
- Dog: moves, breathes, eats
- Rock: stays still
- Tree: grows slowly, leaves move
- Car: moves but doesn't breathe/eat

**Students explore:**
- Click each item
- Watch what it does
- Talk with partner

**Circulate and ask:**
- "What is that thing doing?"
- "Is it alive? How do you know?"

---

### Discovery (10 min)

**Class discussion**

**Ask:**
"What did we discover? What makes something ALIVE?"

**Capture responses on chart:**

Living things:
- 🏃 Move
- 🌬️ Breathe
- 🍎 Need food/water
- 📈 Grow

Non-living:
- ❌ Never alive (rock, car)
- ❌ Can't do those things

**Introduce vocabulary:**
- **Living** - can grow, move, needs
- **Non-Living** - was never alive
- **Needs** - food, water, air, home

---

### Create (10 min)

**Activity: Sorting Cards**

**In pairs:**
1. Get set of 10 picture cards
2. Sort onto "Living" / "Non-Living" chart
3. Add one thing from our classroom to each side

**Cards include:**
- Dog, Cat, Fish, Bird, Tree, Flower (Living)
- Rock, Book, Car, Sun, Cloud, Rain (Non-Living)

**Teacher circulates:**
- Ask: "How do you know?"
- Prompt: "What does this one DO?"

---

### Check (5 min)

**Exit Ticket**

Each student draws:
1. One living thing
2. One non-living thing

**Label each** (or teacher helps)

---

## Differentiation

### Level 1 (Support)
- Pre-sorted examples on desk
- Teacher works with small group
- Ask: "Does it move or grow?"

### Level 2 (Guided)
- Partner sorting
- Sentence stem: "It's living because it _____"

### Level 3 (Extension)
- Add 3rd category: "Was never alive"
- Create own picture card
- "Is fire living? Why/why not?"

## First Peoples Integration

**Connection to BC Curriculum:**

"In many Indigenous cultures, we learn about living things from watching the land. The Secwepemc (Shuswap) people learn about animals, plants, and their relationships from Elders and the land."

- Ask students what they learn from their families about animals/plants
- Connect to local wildlife in BC (deer, eagles, salmon)
- Respect for all living things

---

## Teacher Notes

### Common Misconceptions
- ❌ "The sun is alive" (it moves but doesn't breathe/eat)
- ❌ "Cars are alive" (they move but aren't living)
- ❌ "Seeds aren't alive" (they're waiting to grow!)

### Tips
- Accept all answers at first
- Let students discover through simulation
- Focus on "needs" not just movement
- Make connections to students' pets/families
