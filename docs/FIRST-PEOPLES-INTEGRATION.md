# First Peoples Integration Guide

## What is First Peoples Perspectives?

In BC curriculum, First Peoples perspectives means:
- Including Indigenous knowledge systems
- Connecting to local communities
- Respecting traditional ways of knowing
- Language, stories, and practices

---

## How to Integrate

### Before Lessons
1. Learn whose traditional territory your school is on
2. Connect with local Indigenous education coordinator
3. Find local First Peoples resources

### During Lessons
- Use correct pronunciation of Indigenous names
- Include stories from local communities
- Connect to land-based learning
- Invite Elders or knowledge keepers

### After Lessons
- Reflect on integration
- Get feedback from community

---

## Grade-by-Grade Connections

### Kindergarten
- Local animals in Secwepemc territory
- Stories from local communities
- Plant uses by local First Peoples

### Grade 1
- Local plants and animals
- Traditional uses of materials
- Sky knowledge

### Grade 2
- Life cycles in local ecosystems
- Seasonal rounds
- Traditional foods

### Grade 3
- Biodiversity in local area
- Indigenous land management
- Traditional ecological knowledge

### Grade 4
- Senses in traditional practices
- Materials from the land
- Place-based learning

### Grade 5
- Body systems (traditional healing)
- Rock/mineral uses
- Ecosystem stewardship

## Local Resources (BC)

- First Nations Education Steering Committee (FNESC)
- Indigenous Education Lead in your district
- Local Band education departments
- Elder in Residence programs

---

## Tips

1. **Be authentic** - Don't tokenize, genuinely integrate
2. **Be local** - Use your specific community
3. **Be respectful** - Follow community protocols
4. **Be ongoing** - This is continuous learning
