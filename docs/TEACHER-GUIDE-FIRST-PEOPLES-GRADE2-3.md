# Grade 2-3 Social Studies: BC First Peoples - Teacher Guide

## Unit Overview

**Duration:** 3 weeks (15 lessons)  
**Grades:** 2-3  
**Curriculum Area:** Social Studies / Aboriginal Education

### BC Curriculum Connections
- First Peoples in British Columbia
- Local First Peoples communities and cultures
- Traditions, histories, and contributions

---

## Week 1: Introduction to First Peoples

### Lesson 1: Who Are the First Peoples? (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Understand who are the First Peoples of BC
- Recognize that there are many distinct nations
- Show respect for Indigenous peoples

### Vocabulary
- **First Peoples:** The original peoples of Canada
- **Nation:** A group of people with shared culture and territory
- **Indigenous:** Another word for First Peoples

### Direct Instruction (15 min)

**Key Messages:**
- Indigenous peoples lived in BC for thousands of years before European contact
- There are many distinct First Nations in BC (over 200!)
- Each nation has its own language, traditions, and territory
- First Peoples are still here today with vibrant cultures

### Map Activity (20 min)
Show map of BC with First Nations territories
- Point out local nation(s)
- Discuss: What territory do we live on?

### Respect Circles (15 min)
Discuss:
- How should we learn about First Peoples?
- Why is it important to be respectful?

---

### Lesson 2: The Six Nations of BC (60 min)

### Overview of BC First Nations

**The major language families:**

| Family | Nations | Location |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Coast Salish | Sto:lo, Squamish, Coast Salish | Vancouver, Fraser Valley |
| Interior Salish | Secwepemc, Nlaka'pamux | Interior BC |
| Wakashan | Nuu-chah-nulth, Kwakwaka'wakw | Vancouver Island, Coast |
| Athabascan | Dene, Tahltan | Northern BC |
| Tsimshian | Tsimshian, Gitxsan | Northwest coast |
| Kootenai | Ktunaxa | Southeastern BC |

### Activity: Research Stations

Groups research one nation:
- Location
- Traditional territory
- Language
- Food sources
- Cultural traditions

## Week 2: Traditional Life

### Lesson 3: Housing and Shelter (60 min)

### Types of Traditional Housing

**Coast Nations:**
- Longhouses (large, multi-family)
- Cedar plank houses

**Interior Nations:**
- Pit houses (underground)
- Summer tents (tepees)

### Activity: Compare and Contrast

Create a Venn diagram comparing:
- Coastal vs. Interior housing

### Building Activity
Students build model houses using:
- Craft sticks
- Cardboard
- Paper

---

### Lesson 4: Food and Sustenance (60 min)

### Traditional Foods

**Coastal:**
- Salmon (most important!)
- Shellfish
- Whale
- Berries

**Interior:**
- Deer, elk, moose
- Salmon (where available)
- Roots and berries

### The Salmon Cycle

Teaching about the importance of salmon:
- Salmon are "anadromous" (born in freshwater, live in ocean, return to freshwater to spawn)
- First Nations managed salmon sustainably for thousands of years

### Food Preparation
- Smoking fish
- Drying meat
- Preserving berries

---

### Lesson 5: Transportation (60 min)

### Before Roads

**Water:**
- Canoes (different types for different purposes)
- Cedar dugout canoes
- Large war canoes

**Land:**
- Walking trails
- Horses (after contact)
- Dogsleds

### Activity: Canoe Building

Discuss canoe building:
- Cedar trees are sacred
- Many hands needed
- Traditional techniques

## Week 3: Culture and Traditions

### Lesson 6: Art and Symbolism (60 min)

### Types of Indigenous Art

- **Totem poles:** Story poles with clan animals
- **Masks:** Used in ceremonies
- **Weaving:** Cedar bark blankets, baskets
- **Jewelry:** Silver, copper, shells

### Respectful Art Activities

**Note:** It's okay to learn about and create art inspired by First Nations art, but students should understand:
- It's for learning, not appropriating
- Real totem poles have deep meaning
- We appreciate, we don't claim as our own

### Activity: Design Your Own Totem

Students create a simple design:
- Choose 3 animals
- What does each represent?

---

### Lesson 7: Potlatch and Ceremonies (60 min)

### What is a Potlatch?

- Traditional ceremony
- Gift-giving feast
- Celebrations (weddings, naming, memorials)
- Illegal for many years (government banned it)
- Now celebrated again!

### Key Values
- Sharing wealth
- Respect for elders
- Connecting generations
- Celebrating identity

### Discussion
Why was it wrong to ban the potlatch?

---

### Lesson 8: Connecting to Today (60 min)

### First Peoples Today

**Key Points:**
- First Peoples are still here!
- Many live in communities, cities, and towns
- They hold leadership roles
- Cultures are being preserved and renewed

### Local Connections

Invite a local Elder or Knowledge Keeper to:
- Share traditions
- Answer questions
- Teach a song or story

### Our Responsibility

How can we show respect?
- Learn the true history
- Listen and learn
- Stand against stereotypes
- Support Indigenous-led initiatives

## Important Considerations

### Cultural Protocols
- Always ask permission before sharing
- Don't share stories that aren't meant to be shared
- Show respect for sacred items

### Language
- Learning a few words of local language is a great start
- Use correct pronunciation
- Don't mock or misuse
