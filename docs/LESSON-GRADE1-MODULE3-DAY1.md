# Grade 1 Science - Light and Shadow
## Day 1: Making Shadows

**Duration:** 45 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

1. **Create** a shadow using a light source
2. **Describe** how shadows are made
3. **Explain** why shadows change

---

## Materials

- [ ] Flashlights (1 per pair)
- [ ] Various objects (balls, blocks, hands)
- [ ] White sheets/poster board
- [ ] Chart paper

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 min)

**Hook:**
"It's a sunny day. I want you to stand up and look down."

"Do you see something beneath you?"

"SHADOWS! Today we're going to learn about shadows."

---

### Exploration (15 min)

**Activity: Shadow Play**

In pairs with flashlights:

1. Shine flashlight on wall
2. Hold object between light and wall
3. Move object closer/farther
4. Move flashlight closer/farther

**Teacher asks:**
- "What makes the shadow?"
- "What happens when you move closer?"
- "Does the shadow change size?"

---

### Discovery (10 min)

**Class chart:**

**How Shadows Work:**
- Light travels in a straight line
- When something BLOCKS the light...
- A SHADOW appears!

**Shadow Rules:**
- Big light + close object = BIG shadow
- Small light + far object = SMALL shadow

---

### Create (10 min)

**Shadow Art:**

Make shadow animals on the wall:
- 🦅 Eagle
- 🐕 Dog  
- ✌️ Hand shapes

**Extension:** Make shadow puppets that tell a story

---

### Check (5 min)

Exit ticket: "Draw a shadow and show where the light is"

---

## Differentiation

**Level 1:** Teacher guides flashlight, student holds object
**Level 2:** Partner work
**Level 3:** "How could you make a shadow disappear?"

---

## Assessment

- Participation in exploration
- Understanding of blocking light
- Exit ticket accuracy
