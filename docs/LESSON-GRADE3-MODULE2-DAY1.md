# Grade 3 Science - Forces
## Day 1: Push and Pull

**Duration:** 45 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

1. **Define** push and pull as forces
2. **Identify** pushes and pulls in everyday life
3. **Experiment** with how force affects motion

---

## Materials

- [ ] Balls (various sizes)
- [ ] Toy cars
- [ ] Ramps
- [ ] Spring scales (optional)
- [ ] Chart paper

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 min)

**Hook:**
"I need a volunteer!"

Push the student gently (with warning!)

"Now I pushed you. You MOVED!"

"Today we're learning about FORCES - pushes and pulls!"

---

### Exploration (15 min)

**Activity: Push and Pull Station**

Stations around room:

**Station 1: Balls**
- Push ball
- Pull ball back with other hand
- What's different?

**Station 2: Cars**
- Push car down ramp
- Push car up ramp
- What happens?

**Station 3: Pulling**
- Pull wagon
- Pull with string
- Different?

**Station 4: Magnets**
- Push: Like poles
- Pull: Opposite poles

---

### Discovery (10 min)

**Class discussion:**

**What is a FORCE?**
- A push OR a pull
- Makes things move
- Can speed things up, slow things down, change direction

**Chart:**

| Push | Pull |
|------|------|
| Kicking ball | Pulling wagon |
| Pushing door | Pulling door handle |
| Throwing | Bouncing back |

---

### Create (10 min)

**Draw and Label:**

Students draw 3 pushes and 3 pulls they use every day

**Share with partner**

---

### Check (5 min)

Exit ticket: "Draw a push and a pull"

---

## Assessment

- Understanding of push vs pull
- Participation in experiments
- Real-world connections
