## Lesson Modules (Code-First Inquiry)

### Module 1: "Living Things in Our World" 🌿🐌

**BC Alignment:**
- Science: Living things have features and behaviours that help them survive
- Science: Classification of living/non-living
- ADST: Generate ideas from experiences

**The Mystery:**
> Show a simulation of a snail moving slowly vs. a grasshopper jumping. 
> "How do animals move differently? Why?"

**Code Exploration:**
- Interactive simulation: Animals with different movement patterns
- Students adjust speed, legs, body shape
- Discover: Different features → different movements → survival

**Interactive Elements:**
- Drag to change animal features
- Watch movement patterns emerge
- Compare local BC animals (ladybug, robin, salmon)

**Quick Check (3 questions):**
1. Is a rock living or non-living? How do you know?
2. Name one way animals move.
3. What feature helps a bird fly?

---

### Module 2: "Matter Matters" 💧🪨

**BC Alignment:**
- Science: Matter is useful because of its properties
- Science: Solids keep shape; liquids and gases flow
- Math: Measurement

**The Mystery:**
> Show water behaving differently - sometimes it stays in a ball, sometimes it spreads out.
> "What's happening?"

**Code Exploration:**
- Simulation: Particles behaving as solid/liquid/gas
- Students change temperature, observe state changes
- Discover: Heat = energy = movement

**Interactive Elements:**
- Temperature slider → watch particles speed up/spread
- Click to add "heat"
- Watch ice melt → water → steam

**Quick Check:**
1. What are the three states of matter?
2. What happens to particles when they get hot?
3. Give an example of a solid and a liquid.

---

### Module 3: "Light & Shadow Play" ☀️🌑

**BC Alignment:**
- Science: Light and sound can be produced and their properties can be changed
- Science: Objects are made visible by light or reflection
- Science: Shadows

**The Mystery:**
> Show a shadow that changes size throughout the "day"
> "Why does your shadow change?"

**Code Exploration:**
- Simulation: Sun moving across sky, shadows changing
- Students move light source, observe shadow behavior
- Discover: Light travels in straight lines, blocked = shadow

**Interactive Elements:**
- Move the sun (light source)
- Change time of day
- Make shadow disappear / appear

**Quick Check:**
1. What makes a shadow?
2. Where does the light come from during the day?
3. Can shadows change size? How?

---

### Module 4: "Patterns in the Sky" 🌙⭐

**BC Alignment:**
- Science: Observable patterns and cycles in the sky
- Science: Moon, stars, sunrise/set
- First Peoples: Shared knowledge of the sky
- Math: Patterns, cycles

**The Mystery:**
> Show the moon changing shape over 28 days
> "Why does the moon change?"

**Code Exploration:**
- Simulation: Moon phases animated
- Students click through days, observe pattern
- Discover: The moon doesn't change - our view of it does

**Interactive Elements:**
- Click to advance days
- See moon from different angles
- Connect to BC First Peoples moon stories

**Quick Check:**
1. What shape is the moon?
2. Does the moon make its own light?
3. What pattern do you notice about the moon?

---

### Module 5: "Sound Explorations" 🔊🎵

**BC Alignment:**
- Science: Properties of sound (pitch, volume)
- Science: Ways to make, record, transmit sound
- ADST: Use tools to extend capabilities

**The Mystery:**
> Play two sounds - one high, one low
> "What's different about these sounds?"

**Code Exploration:**
- Simulation: Sound visualizer
- Students adjust frequency, see waves change
- Discover: Fast vibrations = high pitch

**Interactive Elements:**
- Make sounds with different pitches
- See sound waves
- Connect to local animals (bird calls, frog sounds)

**Quick Check:**
1. How can you make a sound?
2. What's different between loud and quiet?
3. What makes a high-pitched sound?

---

### Module 6: "Sorting & Classifying" 🔢

**BC Alignment:**
- Science: Classification of living/non-living
- Math: Sorting, classifying
- ADST: Choose criteria for sorting

**The Mystery:**
> Show a mixed pile - "How would you organize this?"

**Code Exploration:**
- Simulation: Sorting game
- Students create categories, sort objects
- Discover: Different criteria = different organizations

**Interactive Elements:**
- Drag items into categories
- Change sorting rules
- Compare with how scientists classify

**Quick Check:**
1. How do we sort living things?
2. What makes something "living"?
3. Can you sort these in a different way?

## Lesson Structure (Per Module)

### Format: "Code Lab" (45-60 min)

```
Part 1: THE HOOK (5 min)
├── Mystery demo on screen
└── "What do you notice?"

Part 2: THE EXPLORATION (15 min)
├── Students use interactive simulation
├── Tweak parameters
└── Make predictions

Part 3: THE DISCOVERY (10 min)
├── Share findings
├── Connect to BC context
└── Introduce vocabulary

Part 4: THE CREATE (15 min)
├── Guided making activity
├── Apply concepts
└── Share with partner

Part 5: THE CHECK (5 min)
├── 3-question quick check
└── Exit ticket
```
