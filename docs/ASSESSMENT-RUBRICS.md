# Assessment Rubrics - Code-First Inquiry

## General Rubric (All Modules)

### Level 4: Exceeds Expectations
- Explains scientific concepts accurately
- Creates original extensions to activities
- Connects learning to real-world examples
- Helps other students understand

### Level 3: Meets Expectations
- Completes all required activities
- Accurately describes concepts
- Follows instructions appropriately
- Shows understanding of main ideas

### Level 2: Approaching
- Completes most activities with support
- Describes some concepts accurately
- Needs some guidance to complete tasks
- Shows partial understanding

### Level 1: Beginning
- Participates in activities
- With support, identifies main ideas
- Needs significant help to complete tasks

## Module-Specific Rubrics

### Living Things (Grade 1)

| Criteria | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|----------|---|---|---|---|
| Classification | Needs help to sort | Sorts with guidance | Independently sorts correctly | Creates new categories |
| Vocabulary | Few science words | Some science words | Uses key vocabulary | Uses vocabulary accurately |
| Explanations | Cannot explain | Explains with prompting | Explains clearly | Explains and extends |

### Matter (Grade 1)

| Criteria | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|----------|---|---|---|---|
| States | Cannot identify | Names with help | Identifies all 3 | Explains differences |
| Observations | Few observations | Some observations | Detailed observations | Makes predictions |
| Properties | Doesn't describe | Describes with help | Describes accurately | Connects to uses |

## Self-Assessment Cards

### "I Can" Statements (Grade 1)

- [ ] I can sort living and non-living things
- [ ] I can tell you about the 3 states of matter
- [ ] I can make a shadow
- [ ] I can draw the moon
- [ ] I can make different sounds
- [ ] I can sort things in different ways

### Reflection Prompts

1. What surprised you today?
2. What was hard? What helped?
3. What do you want to learn more about?
4. How does this connect to your life?
