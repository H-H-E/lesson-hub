# Grade 1 Science: Living Things
## A Systems Literacy Journey into the Web of Life

---

## 🌿 Systems Literacy Vision Connection

*"You are not a lone creature being bossed around by morality; you are a living process inside other living processes."*

This lesson embraces the Systems Literacy vision by helping Grade 1 students discover that **they are part of a larger living system**—not separate observers of nature, but connected participants in the web of life. Students will explore how living things depend on each other, how they are all processes within larger processes, and how they themselves are living processes connected to family, community, and environment.

---

## 📋 4 SMART Learning Objectives

**By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:**

1. **Identify** at least 5 living things in their classroom or outdoor environment and explain why each is "living" (using the criteria: grows, moves, needs food/water, responds to surroundings).

2. **Describe** how living things are connected to each other by creating a simple "living chain" showing how plants and animals help each other survive.

3. **Compare** the basic needs of humans, animals, and plants (food, water, air, shelter) and explain how these needs are met in their local environment.

4. **Create** a collaborative "Living Things Web" artwork showing themselves as a connected part of the natural world, with at least 3 connections to other living things.

## 🎣 Engaging Hook: The Mystery of the Growing Sprout (2-3 minutes)

### Setup (Before students enter):
Place on a table under a cloth:
- A clear plastic cup with a bean seed planted in soil (visible roots beginning to grow)
- A small rock
- A photograph of a sleeping cat

### The Mystery (Teacher speaks):

**"Class, I have three things under this cloth. Two of them are ALIVE. One is NOT. But I need YOUR help to figure out which is which!"**

Lift the cloth to reveal the three items.

**"Here's the mystery: One of these things is growing right now. One is breathing right now. One has been alive but is now still. Can you point to which one you think is living?"**

Give students 30 seconds to look and point.

**"How did you know? What clues helped you decide?"** (Take 2-3 responses)

**"Today we're going to become Living Thing detectives. By the end of our lesson, you'll be able to tell ANYONE how we know if something is living or not—and you'll discover something amazing: YOU are a living thing too!"**

*Transition signal: Ring the bell three times.*

## 🕐 Complete Lesson Sequence

### Total Time: 60 minutes

---

### PHASE 1: EXPLORE 🔍 (15 minutes)

**Timing: 0:00 - 15:00**

#### Activity 1.1: Living Thing Sort (8 minutes)

**Teacher Instructions:**
1. Scatter picture cards (living and non-living) across the floor or on tables.
2. Divide class into two teams: "Living" and "Non-Living."
3. Students take turns picking a card and placing it on the correct side of the room.

**Key Questions During Activity:**
- "What makes you put that there?"
- "Could it still be living even if it's not moving right now?"
- "What about this seed—is it alive? How do you know?"

**Teacher Note:** Accept all answers at this stage. The goal is to surface students' current thinking.

#### Activity 1.2: The Five Clues Song (7 minutes)

**Teacher Instructions:**
Sing to the tune of "Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes":

*"Living things grow, living things move,*  
*Living things need food—we've got the proof!*  
*Living things breathe, respond to their home,*  
*Living things reproduce—that's how life spreads its clone!"*

**With actions:**
- "grow" → hands up like stretching
- "move" → wiggle fingers
- "need food" → rub tummy
- "breathe" → breathing motion
- "respond" → point to eyes/ears
- "reproduce" → spread fingers like flower opening

*Sing twice, second time faster!*

---

### PHASE 2: DISCOVER 🌱 (18 minutes)

**Timing: 15:00 - 33:00**

#### Activity 2.1: Outdoor Discovery Walk (10 minutes)

**Teacher Instructions:**
1. Take students outside (or to windows/indoor plants).
2. Each student finds ONE living thing and ONE non-living thing.
3. They observe using their magnifying glass.
4. Record in Explorer's Journal: draw what they found and check off which "living clues" it has.

**Explorer's Journal Prompt:**
*"I found a living thing: _______________*
*It shows these signs of being alive: ☐ Grows ☐ Moves ☐ Needs food ☐ Breathes ☐ Responds"*

**Safety Note:** Remind students not to touch or pick up creatures, and to stay within the designated area.

#### Activity 2.2: The Living Chain Game (8 minutes)

**Teacher Instructions:**
1. Students sit in a circle.
2. Give each student a picture card (plant, animal, human, or item).
3. Teacher starts: "I am a human. I need water. Who can give me water?"
4. Student with "rain/cloud/plant" card stands and says: "I am a plant. I give water. I need sunlight."
5. Next student connects: "I am the sun..."
6. Continue until everyone is connected in a chain.

**Key Discussion:**
*"Look at our chain! Could we break it? What would happen if one link was missing?"*

**Teacher Note:** This builds the foundation for understanding systems—everything is connected!

---

### PHASE 3: CREATE 🎨 (15 minutes)

**Timing: 33:00 - 48:00**

#### Activity 3.1: Living Things Web Collage (12 minutes)

**Teacher Instructions:**
1. Give each group (4-5 students) a large piece of paper with a pre-drawn "child" in the center.
2. Students draw/collage connections from themselves to:
   - Family members
   - Pets or classroom animals
   - Plants (trees, grass, food plants)
   - Community helpers who help living things (veterinarian, farmer)
   - The sun, water, air

3. They connect each with a string/line and write what the connection provides.

**Example:**
- "I → Sun → gives me warmth and food"
- "I → Dog → gives me friendship and I give it love"

#### Activity 3.2: Share the Web (3 minutes)

**Groups briefly present one connection they added to their web.**

**Celebration Phrase:** "We are all connected in the web of life!"

---

### PHASE 4: CHECK ✓ (12 minutes)

**Timing: 48:00 - 60:00**

#### Activity 4.1: Exit Ticket - "Thumbs Up, Thumbs Down" (4 minutes)

**Teacher Instructions:**
Read statements. Students show thumbs up (agree) or thumbs down (disagree):

1. "A toy robot is a living thing." (Thumbs down)
2. "A seed is living even if it doesn't move." (Thumbs up)
3. "Plants need water to stay alive." (Thumbs up)
4. "I am part of the living world." (Thumbs up)
5. "A rock is alive because it's outside." (Thumbs down)

#### Activity 4.2: "I Am a Living Thing" Reflection (3 minutes)

**Students complete in Explorer's Journal:**

*"I am a living thing because..."*

*(Teacher circulates and reads responses aloud as formative assessment)*

#### Activity 4.3: Systems Literacy Connection - Closing Circle (5 minutes)

**Teacher Instructions:**
Gather students in a circle.

**"Remember our mystery? Now we know the answer! Let's share one thing we learned."**

Go around quickly (10 seconds each).

**Final Connection:**

**"You know what? That quote we have in our classroom says: 'You are not a lone creature being bossed around by morality; you are a living process inside other living processes.' What do you think this means now?"**

*(Accept 2-3 responses - guide toward: we are part of nature, we are connected to everything)*

**"You are a living process inside many other living processes: your family, your school, your community, the Earth. And all of those processes are inside YOU too. We are all connected!"**

## 🎯 3-Level Differentiation

### Level 1: Highest Support (Scaffolding)

**For students who need more support:**

- **Visuals:** Provide a "Living Things Checklist" with pictures showing each of the 5 characteristics (grow = small seed → big plant; move = bird flying; need food = baby eating; breathe = lungs; respond = child touching a hot stove)
- **Partner Work:** Pair with a more confident student for the outdoor walk
- **Simplified Recording:** Instead of drawing, use stickers or stamps to mark which signs of life they observe
- **Sentence Starters:** Provide incomplete sentences for journal: "I found a ________. It is living because it _________."
- **Extra Teacher Support:** During the Living Chain, give these students the role of "helper" who can hold the end of the yarn

### Level 2: Guided (On-Level)

**For students working at grade level:**

- **Independent Exploration:** Complete outdoor walk with minimal support
- **Written Reflection:** Write 2-3 sentences in journal explaining why something is living
- **Extended Web:** Add 4+ connections in the collaborative web
- **Challenge Option:** During Exit Ticket, students who finish early can explain WHY they gave their answer

### Level 3: Extension (Above-Level)

**For students ready for more challenge:**

- **Research Question:** "Can you find something that is ALIVE but doesn't look alive? What is it?" (Hint: seeds, spores, eggs)
- **System Thinking Prompt:** "What would happen if all the bees disappeared? How would it affect you?"
- **Creation Challenge:** Create their own "Living Things Field Guide" page for one living thing they observed, including: drawing, 3 facts, and one connection to another living thing
- **Extension Connection:** Discuss how they are a "process" inside their family's process, their school's process, Earth's process

## 🇨🇦 First Peoples Principles of Learning Integration

This lesson incorporates the First Peoples Principles of Learning through:

1. **Learning is embedded in memory, history, and story.**
   - *Integration:* Share a brief Indigenous story about the interconnection of all living things (e.g., "The Giving Tree" or a local First Nations story about plants and animals helping each other)

2. **Learning involves recognizing the consequences of one's actions.**
   - *Integration:* Discuss how our choices affect other living things (e.g., caring for plants, not wasting water, being kind to animals)

3. **Learning is holistic, involving the mind, heart, body, and spirit.**
   - *Integration:* The Living Web activity connects students emotionally ("I love my pet"), physically (outdoor walk), mentally (figuring out the mystery), and spiritually (feeling part of something larger)

4. **Learning is relational and involves recognizing the connections among people.**
   - *Integration:* The Living Chain and Web activities explicitly show relationships between living things, including the relationship between students and the land

5. **Learning is experiential and tied to the land.**
   - *Integration:* The outdoor discovery walk connects students to the actual living things in their immediate environment

6. **Learning respects the cycle of life and involves understanding our place in the generations.**
   - *Integration:* Discuss how plants grow from seeds (like us growing up), and how we are part of a chain of generations of living things

## 💻 p5.js Simulation Code

A hands-on interactive simulation for students to explore living things digitally. This code runs in editor.p5js.org.

**Copy and paste the following code into editor.p5js.org to run the simulation:**

```javascript
// Living Things Simulation - Grade 1 Science
// A gentle introduction to the web of life

let plants = [];
let animals = [];
let sun;
let cloud;
let player;
let gameState = "start"; // start, explore, game
let instructions = "Click anywhere to start exploring!";

function setup() {
  createCanvas(600, 400);
  textSize(16);
  textAlign(CENTER, CENTER);
  
  // Create the sun
  sun = new Sun(500, 60);
  
  // Create clouds
  cloud = new Cloud(100, 80);
  
  // Create some plants
  plants.push(new Plant(80, 300, "flower"));
  plants.push(new Plant(200, 320, "grass"));
  plants.push(new Plant(350, 290, "tree"));
  plants.push(new Plant(500, 310, "bush"));
  
  // Create some animals
  animals.push(new Animal(150, 350, "butterfly", "flutter"));
  animals.push(new Animal(300, 360, "rabbit", "hop"));
  animals.push(new Animal(450, 340, "bird", "fly"));
  animals.push(new Animal(250, 280, "bee", "buzz"));
  
  // Create the player character (representing the student)
  player = new Player(300, 200);
}

function draw() {
  background(135, 206, 235); // Sky blue
  
  // Draw ground
  noStroke();
  fill(34, 139, 34); // Green grass
  rect(0, 250, width, 150);
  
  // Title and instructions
  fill(255);
  stroke(0);
  strokeWeight(2);
  textSize(24);
  text("🌱 Living Things Explorer 🌱", width/2, 30);
  textSize(14);
  text(instructions, width/2, 55);
  
  // Draw sun
  sun.display();
  
  // Draw clouds
  cloud.display();
  cloud.move();
  
  // Draw plants
  for (let plant of plants) {
    plant.display();
  }
  
  // Draw animals
  for (let animal of animals) {
    animal.display();
    animal.move();
    animal.checkConnection(plants);
  }
  
  // Draw player
  player.display();
  player.move();
  
  // Display info when clicking on things
  displayInfo();
}

function mousePressed() {
  if (gameState === "start") {
    gameState = "explore";
    instructions = "Move with arrow keys. Click on things to learn about them!";
  } else {
    // Check what player clicked on
    checkClick(mouseX, mouseY);
  }
}

function displayInfo() {
  fill(255);
  noStroke();
  rect(10, 350, 180, 40, 10);
  fill(0);
  textSize(12);
  textAlign(LEFT, CENTER);
  text("Use arrow keys to move!", 20, 370);
  textAlign(CENTER, CENTER);
}

function checkClick(x, y) {
  // Check if clicked on plants
  for (let plant of plants) {
    if (plant.isClicked(x, y)) {
      instructions = plant.name + ": " + plant.fact;
    }
  }
  
  // Check if clicked on animals
  for (let animal of animals) {
    if (animal.isClicked(x, y)) {
      instructions = animal.name + ": " + animal.fact;
    }
  }
  
  // Check sun
  if (sun.isClicked(x, y)) {
    instructions = "Sun: Gives light and warmth to ALL living things!";
  }
  
  // Check player
  if (player.isClicked(x, y)) {
    instructions = "YOU are a living thing! You are part of this system!";
  }
}

// Classes
class Sun {
  constructor(x, y) {
    this.x = x;
    this.y = y;
    this.size = 50;
  }
  
  display() {
    fill(255, 215, 0);
    stroke(255, 165, 0);
    strokeWeight(3);
    ellipse(this.x, this.y, this.size);
    
    // Rays
    for (let i = 0; i < 8; i++) {
      let angle = i * TWO_PI / 8 + frameCount * 0.01;
      let rayLen = 20 + sin(frameCount * 0.05) * 5;
      line(this.x + cos(angle) * 30, this.y + sin(angle) * 30,
           this.x + cos(angle) * (30 + rayLen), this.y + sin(angle) * (30 + rayLen));
    }
  }
  
  isClicked(x, y) {
    return dist(x, y, this.x, this.y) < this.size;
  }
}

class Cloud {
  constructor(x, y) {
    this.x = x;
    this.y = y;
  }
  
  display() {
    noStroke();
    fill(255, 255, 255, 200);
    ellipse(this.x, this.y, 60, 30);
    ellipse(this.x + 20, this.y - 10, 50, 35);
    ellipse(this.x - 20, this.y + 5, 45, 25);
  }
  
  move() {
    this.x += 0.3;
    if (this.x > width + 50) this.x = -50;
  }
}

class Plant {
  constructor(x, y, type) {
    this.x = x;
    this.y = y;
    this.type = type;
    
    if (type === "flower") {
      this.name = "Flower";
      this.fact = "Flowers make seeds for new plants!";
      this.color = [255, 182, 193];
    } else if (type === "grass") {
      this.name = "Grass";
      this.fact = "Grass makes oxygen for us to breathe!";
      this.color = [50, 205, 50];
    } else if (type === "tree") {
      this.name = "Tree";
      this.fact = "Trees give homes to birds and squirrels!";
      this.color = [34, 139, 34];
    } else {
      this.name = "Bush";
      this.fact = "Bushes give shelter to small animals!";
      this.color = [0, 100, 0];
    }
  }
  
  display() {
    fill(this.color[0], this.color[1], this.color[2]);
    stroke(0);
    strokeWeight(1);
    
    if (this.type === "tree") {
      // Tree trunk
      fill(139, 69, 19);
      rect(this.x - 5, this.y, 10, 30);
      // Tree top
      fill(this.color[0], this.color[1], this.color[2]);
      ellipse(this.x, this.y - 10, 40, 50);
    } else if (this.type === "bush") {
      ellipse(this.x, this.y, 35, 25);
    } else if (this.type === "flower") {
      // Stem
      stroke(0, 100, 0);
      line(this.x, this.y, this.x, this.y + 20);
      noStroke();
      // Flower
      fill(this.color[0], this.color[1], this.color[2]);
      ellipse(this.x, this.y, 15, 15);
      fill(255, 255, 0);
      ellipse(this.x, this.y, 5, 5);
    } else {
      // Grass
      for (let i = -2; i <= 2; i++) {
        stroke(34, 139, 34);
        line(this.x + i * 5, this.y, this.x + i * 5 + random(-3, 3), this.y - 15);
      }
    }
  }
  
  isClicked(x, y) {
    return x > this.x - 25 && x < this.x + 25 && y > this.y - 30 && y < this.y + 35;
  }
}

class Animal {
  constructor(x, y, type, movement) {
    this.x = x;
    this.y = y;
    this.type = type;
    this.movement = movement;
    this.startX = x;
    this.direction = 1;
    this.frameOffset = random(100);
    
    if (type === "butterfly") {
      this.name = "Butterfly";
      this.fact = "Butterflies help flowers make seeds!";
      this.color = [255, 165, 0];
    } else if (type === "rabbit") {
      this.name = "Rabbit";
      this.fact = "Rabbits eat plants and are food for other animals!";
      this.color = [192, 192, 192];
    } else if (type === "bird") {
      this.name = "Bird";
      this.fact = "Birds eat seeds and insects, and spread plant seeds!";
      this.color = [135, 206, 250];
    } else {
      this.name = "Bee";
      this.fact = "Bees help flowers make fruit by spreading pollen!";
      this.color = [255, 215, 0];
    }
  }
  
  display() {
    fill(this.color[0], this.color[1], this.color[2]);
    stroke(0);
    strokeWeight(1);
    
    let bounce = sin((frameCount + this.frameOffset) * 0.1) * 5;
    
    if (this.type === "butterfly") {
      let wingFlap = sin((frameCount + this.frameOffset) * 0.3) * 20;
      ellipse(this.x - 8, this.y + bounce, 12 + wingFlap, 10);
      ellipse(this.x + 8, this.y + bounce, 12 + wingFlap, 10);
      ellipse(this.x, this.y + bounce, 8, 12);
    } else if (this.type === "rabbit") {
      ellipse(this.x, this.y + bounce, 20, 15); // body
```

## 🚀 3 Extension Activities

### Extension 1: "My Living Thing Friend" Observation Journal (Ongoing)

**Duration:** 2 weeks (10-15 minutes daily)

**Description:** Each student chooses one living thing to observe over two weeks. This could be:
- A plant in the classroom or at home
- A class pet
- A pet at home
- A tree outside their window

**Materials:** Observation journal template, magnifying glass, ruler

**Process:**
1. Day 1: Draw the living thing and write what it looks like
2. Days 2-5: Draw/describe any changes noticed
3. Days 6-10: Measure and record growth/changes
4. Final day: Create a "Profile Page" for their living thing friend

**Connection to Systems Literacy:** Helps students see themselves as observers who are part of the living process they're studying

---

### Extension 2: "Living Systems Detective" Research Project

**Duration:** 1 week (30 minutes per day)

**Description:** Students become "Living Systems Detectives" to investigate one connection in the living world. Each student (or pair) investigates a specific question:

- "How are bees connected to apples?"
- "How are trees connected to birds?"
- "How are we connected to the food we eat?"
- "How is rain connected to plants?"

**Process:**
1. Day 1: Choose a question and draw what you think the connection is
2. Day 2: Look at books/websites to find the answer
3. Day 3: Create a "connection poster" showing the relationship
4. Day 4: Present to the class
5. Day 5: Connect all posters to create a class "Web of Life"

**Connection to Systems Literacy:** Directly demonstrates "you are a living process inside other living processes" by showing how everything relates

---

### Extension 3: "Systems Helper" Action Project

**Duration:** Ongoing (30 minutes weekly for 4 weeks)

**Description:** Students take action to help the living systems they're part of. Choose one action per week:

**Week 1: Water Saver**
- Learn why water is important for all living things
- Create a "Water Saver" chart for home
- Track water-saving actions for one week

**Week 2: Plant Helper**
- Plant seeds in the classroom or school garden
- Care for plants throughout the week
- Document growth and share with class

**Week 3: Animal Helper**
- Create bird feeders or bug hotels
- Learn about helping local wildlife
- Design a "Kind to Animals" promise

**Week 4: Community Helper**
- Share what they've learned with family
- Create a poster to teach younger students
- Reflect on how their actions help the living system

**Connection to Systems Literacy:** Shows students that they are active participants in living systems—not just observers—and their actions matter
