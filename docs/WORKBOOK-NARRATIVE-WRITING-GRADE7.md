## Unit Overview

In this unit, you will learn to write amazing stories! By the end, you'll have your very own published short story.

---

## Week 1: Understanding Stories

### What Makes a Good Story?

Every great story has these ingredients:

1. **Characters** - Who is in the story?
2. **Setting** - Where and when does it take place?
3. **Conflict** - What is the problem?
4. **Plot** - What happens? (Beginning, middle, end)
5. **Resolution** - How is the problem solved?

## Week 4: Writing Your Draft

### Writing Tips

✓ Use sensory details (what do you see, hear, smell, taste, feel?)
✓ Show, don't tell (instead of "She was sad," write "Tears rolled down her cheeks")
✓ Add dialogue to bring characters to life
✓ Use interesting words (big → enormous, huge, massive)
✓ Vary sentence lengths

### Drafting Checklist

- [ ] My story has a clear beginning
- [ ] I introduced my main character
- [ ] There is a problem the character faces
- [ ] The problem gets bigger/more complicated
- [ ] There is an exciting climax
- [ ] The story ends with a resolution
- [ ] I used dialogue
- [ ] I added sensory details

---

## Week 5: Revising

### Peer Review Questions

1. Is the beginning interesting? What hooked you?
2. Did you understand the setting?
3. Are the characters clear?
4. Was there a problem to solve?
5. Did the ending make sense?
6. What was your favorite part? Why?
7. What is one thing that could be improved?

### Revision Strategies

- **Add** - details, dialogue, descriptions
- **Delete** - unnecessary words,重复
- **Rearrange** - move sentences or paragraphs
- **Replace** - boring words with exciting ones

## Publishing Options

1. **Class Anthology** - All stories bound into one book
2. **Reading Celebration** - Share stories with another class
3. **Digital Publication** - Create a class blog or e-book
4. **Author Chair** - Read aloud to the class
