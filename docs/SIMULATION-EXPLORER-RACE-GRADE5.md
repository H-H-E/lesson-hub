# Grade 5 Social Studies: Early Explorers - Simulation Activity

## "Explorer Race" Simulation

### Teacher Overview

**Time:** 2 class periods (90 min)  
**Grades:** 5-6  
**Group Size:** 4-5 students per group

---

## Learning Goals

- Understand why European explorers came to North America
- Identify challenges explorers faced
- Compare different explorers and their achievements
- Practice decision-making and collaboration

---

## Materials

- Explorer cards (provided)
- Map of North America (blank)
- "Supply Ship" tokens
- Scenario cards
- Dice (one per group)
- Student journals

---

## Part 1: The Backstory (20 min)

### Class Discussion

**Why did Europeans want to explore?**
- New trade routes to Asia
- Wealth and riches
- New lands to claim
- Spread of religion
- Adventure and fame

**What challenges might they face?**
- Unknown lands
- Harsh weather
- Sickness
- Hostile environments
- Running out of supplies

---

## Part 2: Create Your Explorer Profile (30 min)

Each group chooses an explorer and creates their profile:

### Explorer Options

| Explorer | Country | Year | Goal |
|----------|---------|------|------|
| John Cabot | England | 1497 | Find route to Asia |
| Jacques Cartier | France | 1535 | Find gold/river |
| Henry Hudson | England/Netherlands | 1610 | Find Northwest Passage |
| Marco Polo (review) | Italy | 1271 | Trade with China |

### Group Tasks

1. Choose your explorer
2. Create a flag/symbol
3. Decide what supplies to bring (limited to 10 items)
4. Name your ship
5. Set your goal

### Supply List (choose 10)

- ⛵ Ship
- 🗺️ Maps
- 🗡️ Weapons
- 🍞 Food
- 💊 Medicine
- 🔧 Tools
- 📜 Trade goods
- 🧭 Compass
- 👕 Warm clothes
- ⛑️ Armor
- 🔥 Fire-starting kit
- 📿 Gifts for locals

---

## Part 3: The Race! (40 min)

### Game Board Setup

Create a simple path across the map:
- Start: Europe
- Checkpoints: Iceland, Greenland, East Coast Canada, Interior
- End: Pacific (goal)

### Turn Sequence

**Each turn:**
1. Roll dice to move (1-3 spaces)
2. Draw a scenario card
3. Make a decision
4. Record in journal

### Scenario Cards

| Card | Situation | Choice | Result |
|------|-----------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Storm at sea! | Ride it out / Change course | Lose 1 supply / -1 space |
| 2 | Native people | Trade / Fight / Avoid | Gain supplies / Lose supplies / Safe |
| 3 | Sickness! | Use medicine / Keep going | -1 medicine / Lose crew member |
| 4 | Gold found! | Take it / Leave it | Rich! / Nothing |
| 5 | Winter comes | Stay / Keep going | Lose supplies / -2 spaces |
| 6 | Ship damaged | Fix / Abandon | Lose supplies / Turn ends |

### Winning

First group to reach the Pacific OR group with most points when time runs out wins.

---

## Part 4: Debrief (30 min)

### Journal Reflection

Students answer:
1. What was the hardest decision you made?
2. What would you do differently?
3. What surprised you about exploring?

### Class Discussion

- How is exploring today different from long ago?
- What qualities make a good explorer?
- How did exploration change the world?

---

## Assessment

| Criteria | Excellent | Good | Needs Work |
|----------|-----------|------|------------|
| Participation | Led discussions | Contributed occasionally | Rarely participated |
| Decision-making | Thoughtful choices | Made reasonable choices | Random choices |
| Journal | Detailed entries | Basic entries | Incomplete entries |
| Collaboration | Worked well with all | Worked with most | Struggled to work together |

---

## Differentiation

### For Struggling Learners
- Provide decision scaffolds
- Partner with strong student
- Simplify scenarios

### For Advanced Learners
- Add economics (trade, profit/loss)
- Research real explorer journals
- Create additional scenarios

---

## Extensions

- **Writing:** Write a journal entry "from the ship"
- **Art:** Draw the ship or map
- **Research:** Compare different explorers
