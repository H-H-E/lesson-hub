# Grade 5 Science - Module 2
## Human Body Systems

**Duration:** 60 minutes (Lesson 1 of 5)
**Grade:** 5
**Curriculum Area:** Science - Life Sciences

---

## Learning Objectives (SMART)

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

1. **Label** the major organs of the digestive, circulatory, and respiratory systems on a diagram with 90% accuracy
2. **Explain** the function of each body system using complete sentences and scientific vocabulary
3. **Trace** the pathway of food, blood, or air through the body using a flow chart
4. **Analyze** how the three systems work together to maintain homeostasis with 80% accuracy

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (10 min)
**Hook - The Breath Challenge:**
- Have students hold their breath and count how long they can
- Discuss: "What happened in your body when you held your breath?"
- Why couldn't you hold it forever?

*Teacher sets purpose:* "Today we're going to explore how your body is like a team - with different systems that all work together. Indigenous peoples understood this interconnectedness long before modern science."

**Brainstorm:** What do you already know about your body systems?

### Exploration (20 min)
**Body Systems Stations - Jigsaw Activity**

Divide class into 3 groups. Each group becomes "expert" on one system, then teaches others.

**Group A: Digestive System Station**
- Video or poster showing journey of food
- Organs: mouth, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine
- Function: breaks down food into nutrients
- Activity: Label digestive system diagram

**Group B: Circulatory System Station**
- Heart model or video
- Organs: heart, blood vessels (arteries, veins, capillaries), blood
- Function: carries nutrients and oxygen to the body
- Activity: Trace pathway of blood through the heart

**Group C: Respiratory System Station**
- Lung model demonstration
- Organs: nose, trachea, bronchi, lungs
- Function: brings oxygen into body, removes carbon dioxide
- Activity: Label respiratory system

*Connection to First Peoples:* "Traditional Indigenous healing practices often looked at the whole person - the Anishinaabe (Ojibwe) concept of *minobimaatisiiwin* (the good life) includes physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual health. Many First Peoples had sophisticated knowledge of medicinal plants that work with the body's systems."

### Discovery (15 min)
**Whole Class: Systems Working Together**

Using a large body diagram, demonstrate how the three systems interconnect:

**The Connection Story:**
1. You eat an apple (Digestive)
2. Nutrients enter bloodstream (Circulatory)
3. Oxygen from lungs enters bloodstream (Respiratory)
4. Blood carries both to all body cells

**Interactive Diagram:** Students use yarn or pipe cleaners to trace the connections between systems on a poster.

### Create (10 min)
**Activity: Body Systems Comic Strip**

Students create a 4-panel comic showing:
1. A food item entering the body
2. The digestive system breaking it down
3. Nutrients entering the bloodstream
4. The circulatory system delivering nutrients with oxygen

Must label at least 5 organs correctly.

### Check (5 min)
**Exit Ticket:**
- Name one organ in each system (digestive, circulatory, respiratory)
- Explain in one sentence how two systems work together

## Differentiation

### Level 1 - Support
- Pre-labeled diagrams for tracing
- Vocabulary cards with pictures and definitions
- Sentence stems for written work
- Work with a partner during station rotation
- Allow oral response alternatives
- Simplified comic strip template (fewer panels)

### Level 2 - Guided
- Provide partially completed diagrams
- Offer choice of system to focus on for comic
- Include word banks for labeling
- Allow collaborative note-taking
- Check understanding at each station transition

### Level 3 - Extension
- Research additional body systems (nervous, skeletal, muscular)
- Investigate how exercise affects all three systems
- Compare human body systems to another animal
- Research traditional Indigenous medicines and their effects on body systems

---

## Assessment

### Formative Assessment
- **Station Participation:** Observation checklist for collaborative work
- **Labeling Accuracy:** Check diagrams during and after stations
- **Comic Strip:** Evaluate for accuracy and connections between systems
- **Exit Ticket:** Quick check of basic understanding

### Summative Assessment Options
- **Option A:** Body Systems Poster - Create a detailed poster showing all three systems with functions
- **Option B:** Body Systems Test - Written assessment with diagrams and short answers
- **Option C:** "A Day in the Life" - Write a story from the perspective of a nutrient traveling through the body

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Correctly identifies major organs in each system
- [ ] Explains function of each system accurately
- [ ] Shows understanding of how systems work together
- [ ] Uses scientific vocabulary appropriately

## First Peoples Integration

### Content Connections
- **Traditional Medicine Knowledge:** Many Indigenous cultures have extensive knowledge of how the body works and natural remedies
- **Medicinal Plants:** Plants like sage, cedar, and sweetgrass were used traditionally (often in conjunction with understanding body systems)
- **Holistic Health:** Indigenous perspectives often view health as balance between physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual

### Indigenous Knowledge Integration
1. **Haudenosaunee:** Traditional healers understood the interconnectedness of body systems
2. **Coast Salish:** Knowledge of marine foods and their benefits for body health
3. **Interior Salish:** Traditional diets and understanding of digestion

### Respectful Approaches
- Frame Indigenous health knowledge with respect - it's living knowledge, not just history
- Emphasize that Western science is validating what Indigenous peoples knew for centuries
- Connect to current Indigenous health initiatives and community health representatives
- Avoid stereotypes - Indigenous peoples have diverse health practices

### Guest Speaker Suggestion
- Invite a community health representative or Elder to discuss traditional approaches to health and wellness
