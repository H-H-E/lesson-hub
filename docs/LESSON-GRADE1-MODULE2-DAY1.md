# Grade 1 Science - Matter
## Day 1: Solid, Liquid, Gas?

**Duration:** 45 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

1. **Name** the 3 states of matter with examples
2. **Observe** what happens when ice melts
3. **Predict** what will happen with heat

---

## Materials

- [ ] Ice cubes (10 per student)
- [ ] Clear cups
- [ ] Warming tray or sunny window
- [ ] Chart paper
- [ ] "Matter" vocabulary cards

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 min)

**Hook:**
"Today we're going to do an experiment. Close your eyes."

Put ice cube in student's hand.

"Open your eyes. What do you feel?"

**Discuss:**
- Cold
- Wet
- It's melting!

"Science word: This is WATER. It's a SOLID right now. But wait..."

---

### Exploration (15 min)

**Experiment: Ice to Water**

**Setup:**
Each student has:
- Ice cube on cup
- Worksheet to draw

**Teacher says:**
"Watch carefully. Draw what you see at the start, then every 5 minutes."

**What to observe:**
- Ice: hard, cold, keeps shape
- Water: cold, wet, takes cup shape
- Change: solid → liquid

**Circulate and ask:**
- "What's happening to the ice?"
- "Where did it go?"
- "Is it still water?"

---

### Discovery (10 min)

**Class discussion:**

**Draw on chart:**

| State | What I See | What It Does |
|-------|------------|--------------|
| SOLID (Ice) | Hard, white | Keeps its shape |
| LIQUID (Water) | Wet, clear | Takes cup shape |
| GAS (Steam) | Can't see! | Goes into air |

**Key vocabulary:**
- **Solid** - keeps its shape (rock, ice, wood)
- **Liquid** - flows, takes shape (water, juice)
- **Gas** - spreads out (air, steam, clouds)

---

### Create (10 min)

**Draw and label:**
1. Draw ice cube → SOLID
2. Draw melting → ?
3. Draw water → LIQUID

**Extension:** "What would happen if we HEATED the water more?"

---

### Check (5 min)

Exit ticket: "Draw one solid and one liquid"

---

## Differentiation

**Level 1:** Teacher draws with student, provides words
**Level 2:** Partner draw together
**Level 3:** "What would happen to juice in the freezer?"

---

## Assessment

- Observation during experiment
- Drawing accuracy
- Verbal explanation

---

## First Peoples Connection

"In traditional Indigenous knowledge, water is sacred. The Secwepemc people understand water as essential to all life."

- Discuss: How do we take care of water?
- Why is water important to all living things?
