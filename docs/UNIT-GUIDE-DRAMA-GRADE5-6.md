# Grade 5-6 Drama: Storytelling Through Theatre - Unit Guide

## Unit Overview

**Duration:** 6 weeks  
**Grades:** 5-6  
**Curriculum Area:** Arts Education - Drama

### Learning Goals
- Develop confidence in expressing ideas through drama
- Understand theatrical elements
- Collaborate effectively in ensemble
- Use body, voice, and imagination

---

## Week 1: Drama Games and Warm-Ups

### Daily Warm-Up Games

**1. Zip, Zap, Zop**
- Students stand in circle
- Point and say: "Zip!"
- Next person: "Zap!"
- Next: "Zop!"
- Speed up!

**2. Word-at-a-Time Story**
- Group creates story one word at a time
- Go around circle
- Try to make sense!

**3. Freeze**
- Music plays, students move
- Freeze when music stops
- Hold pose until music starts

**4. Yes, Let's!**
- Leader suggests action: "Let's fly!"
- Everyone: "Yes, let's!" and does it
- Take turns leading

---

## Week 2: Characters and Emotions

### Emotion Charades

**Step 1:** Write emotions on cards:
- Happy, Sad, Angry, Scared
- Excited, Bored, Surprised, Confused

**Step 2:** Students pick card and act out emotion (1 min)

**Step 3:** Class guesses!

### Character Walks

- Walk as different characters:
  - Old person
  - Tired person
  - Happy child
  - Boss
  - Robot

- Add status: high status vs. low status

---

## Week 3: Tableau (Frozen Pictures)

### What is Tableau?
Creating a frozen picture to tell a story with your body!

### Steps
1. Choose a story or scene
2. Decide: Who is in the picture?
3. What are they doing?
4. Pose!
5. Add levels (high, middle, low)

### Practice Scenes
- The last day of school
- A family dinner
- Getting ready for a party
- Lost in the forest

---

## Week 4: Script Work

### Reading Scripts

**Elements of a Script:**
- Characters' names
- Dialogue (what they say)
- Stage directions (in brackets)

### Practice Script

```
THE LOST DOG

[Scene: A park. SARAH is sitting on a bench.]

SARAH: (sadly) I miss my dog so much. He's been gone for three days.

[Enter MAX, holding a leash]

MAX: Excuse me! Is this your dog? I found him near the fountain!

SARAH: (happily) Buster! Oh, thank you!

MAX: No problem! I'm glad I found you both!

[End scene]
```

### Students Write Scripts
In groups of 3-4, write a simple scene about:
- Finding something lost
- A disagreement between friends
- An unexpected visitor

---

## Week 5: Rehearsal and Performance Prep

### Rehearsal Tips

- Practice your lines
- Project your voice
- Look at your scene partner
- Use expression!
- Remember: The show must go on!

### Performance Checklist

- [ ] Know your lines
- [ ] Know where to stand
- [ ] Know your emotions
- [ ] Speak loudly
- [ ] Stay in character

---

## Week 6: Performance and Reflection

### Class Performance

Each group performs their scene for the class!

### Audience Skills
- Watch quietly
- Applaud after each performance
- Give positive feedback

### Reflection

Answer:
1. What was your favorite part?
2. What was challenging?
3. What did you improve on?
4. What would you do differently?

## Assessment

### Process Rubric

| Criteria | 4 - Excellent | 3 - Good | 2 - Satisfactory | 1 - Needs Work |
|----------|---------------|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Collaboration | Always works with others | Usually works well | Sometimes contributes | Needs direction |
| Creativity | Very imaginative | Some new ideas | Standard approach | Needs prompting |
| Confidence | Strong presence | Shows some comfort | Occasionally shy | Uncomfortable |
| Skills | Uses voice/body effectively | Developing skills | Basic skills | Limited skills |

### Final Performance Rubric
- Memorization (25%)
- Expression (25%)
- Voice projection (25%)
- Collaboration (25%)

---

## Differentiation

### For English Language Learners
- Allow script use during performance
- Partner support
- Visual cues

### For Struggling Students
- Smaller groups
- Simpler scripts
- More rehearsal time

### For Advanced Students
- Improvise additional scenes
- Direct other students
- Add music or props
