# Differentiation Guide

## Supporting Diverse Learners

### ESL/ELL Students

**Strategies:**
- Visual supports (pictures, videos)
- Partner with confident speaker
- Allow native language discussion first
- Focus on concepts over vocabulary
- Use hands-on activities

**Grade 1 Supports:**
- Picture cards for all vocabulary
- Word banks on worksheets
- Demonstrate before asking
- Accept verbal responses initially

---

### Students with IEPs

**Strategies:**
- Simplified instructions (1-2 steps)
- Extra time
- Quiet workspace available
- Frequent check-ins
- Break into smaller chunks

**Sample Accommodations:**
- Reduced choices on assessments
- Oral vs written responses
- Teacher reads aloud
- Movement breaks

---

### Advanced Learners

**Extension Activities:**
- Research project on topic
- Create own quiz for classmates
- Design extension to simulation
- Lead a small group

**Questions to Push Thinking:**
- What would happen if...?
- Why does that work that way?
- Can you teach this to someone?

---

## Universal Design

### Multiple Entry Points
- Visual: Simulations, diagrams
- Auditory: Discussion, recordings  
- Kinesthetic: Hands-on, movement

### Multiple Means of Expression
- Draw
- Write
- Tell
- Act out
- Build
