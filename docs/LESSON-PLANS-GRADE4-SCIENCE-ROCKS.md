# Grade 4 Science: Rocks and Minerals - Lesson Plans

## Unit Overview

**Duration:** 2 weeks (10 lessons)  
**Grade:** 4  
**Curriculum Area:** Science - Earth Sciences

### BC Curriculum Connections
- Rocks and minerals have distinct characteristics
- Rocks change through natural processes
- Earth materials provide resources

---

## Lesson 1: What is a Rock? (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Define rock and mineral
- Observe and describe rock properties

### Materials
- Collection of rocks (various sizes, colors, textures)
- Magnifying glasses
- Rock observation worksheet

### Hook (5 min)
Show students three different rocks. "Are these all the same? How are they different?"

### Direct Instruction (15 min)
- **Rock:** A solid material made of minerals
- **Mineral:** Natural substance that is not plant or animal

**Properties to observe:**
- Color
- Texture (rough/smooth)
- Pattern/layers
- Weight (light/heavy)
- Shiny or dull

### Activity (30 min)
Students examine 3-4 rocks and complete observation chart:

| My Rock | Color | Texture | Pattern | Other Notes |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|-------------|
| #1 | | | | |
| #2 | | | | |
| #3 | | | | |

### Wrap-up (10 min)
- Share observations
- What properties did you notice?

## Lesson 2: Three Types of Rocks (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Identify igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks
- Explain how each type forms

### Direct Instruction (15 min)

**1. Igneous Rocks**
- Formed from cooled lava or magma
- Examples: Granite, Obsidian, Pumice
- "Igneous" means "from fire"

**2. Sedimentary Rocks**
- Formed from layers of sand and mud pressed together
- Examples: Sandstone, Limestone, Shale
- Often have layers!

**3. Metamorphic Rocks**
- Changed by heat and pressure underground
- Examples: Marble, Slate, Gneiss
- "Meta" means "changed"

### Activity (30 min)
Rock Sort: Groups classify rock samples into the three types.

### Create a Book (15 min)
Students create a "Rock Type Book" with drawings and descriptions.

## Lesson 3: Mineral Properties (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Test and identify mineral properties
- Use tests to identify unknown minerals

### Testing Stations

**1. Hardness Test**
- Scratch with fingernail, penny, or nail
- Scale: 1 (soft) to 10 (hard)

**2. Color Test**
- Look at the outside and inside

**3. Streak Test**
- Rub on unglazed ceramic tile
- See what color streak is left

**4. Luster Test**
- Shiny like metal (metallic)
- Shiny but not metallic (vitreous)
- Dull (earthy)

### Mineral Identification Lab
Students identify mystery minerals using properties!

## Lesson 4: The Rock Cycle (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Understand how rocks change from one type to another

### Hook (5 min)
"Can rocks move? Can they change?"

### Demonstration
Use crayons to demonstrate the rock cycle:
1. Break crayons (sediment)
2. Press together (pressure = sedimentary)
3. Melt with heat (magma)
4. Cool (igneous)

### Activity (30 min)
Create a Rock Cycle diagram showing:
- Heat + Pressure → Metamorphic
- Melting → Igneous
- Weathering → Sedimentary

### Song
(to the tune of "She'll Be Coming Round the Mountain")

```
Rocks will go through changes,
Yes, rocks will go through changes,
Rocks will go through changes,
All day long!
```

## Lesson 5: Rocks and Minerals in BC (60 min)

### Focus on BC Resources

**BC Mining and Resources:**
- Gold in the Cariboo
- Copper in Highland Valley
- Limestone on Vancouver Island

### Research Project (30 min)
Students research one BC rock or mineral:
- Where is it found?
- What is it used for?
- How is it extracted?

### Presentations (30 min)
Share findings with class!

## Lesson 6-7: Soil and Erosion (2 x 60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Understand how rocks become soil
- Learn about erosion

### Demonstration
"Making Soil"
- Put rocks in jar with water
- Shake!
- Let settle
- Observe layers

### Erosion Experiment
Create a "hillside" with different coverings:
- Grass
- Dirt
- Rocks
- Nothing

Pour water and observe erosion!

## Lesson 8-9: Human Use of Earth Materials (2 x 60 min)

### Discussion
- What do we use rocks and minerals for?

### Design Challenge
Students design a product using earth materials:
- What will you make?
- What materials will you use?
- Draw your design
