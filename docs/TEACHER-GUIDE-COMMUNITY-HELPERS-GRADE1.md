# Teacher Guide: Community Helpers - Grade 1 Social Studies

## Unit Overview

**Duration:** 1 week (5 lessons)  
**Grade Level:** Grade 1  
**Curriculum Area:** Social Studies - Communities

### Big Ideas
- People have different roles and responsibilities in their community
- Working together helps communities thrive

---

## Lesson 1: Who Helps Our Community? (45 min)

### Learning Goals
- Identify at least 5 community helpers
- Describe what each helper does

### Materials
- Chart paper
- Community helper pictures
- Crayons and paper

### Lesson Sequence

**Hook (5 min):** "Who helps you at school? Who helps you at home?"

**Direct Instruction (15 min):**
- Introduce 5 key helpers: doctor, police officer, firefighter, teacher, farmer
- Show pictures and discuss their roles
- Create a class web on chart paper

**Guided Practice (15 min):**
- Small groups match helpers to their tools/uniforms

**Independent Practice (10 min):**
- Students draw their favorite community helper

---

## Lesson 2: Visiting Helpers (Field Trip or Guest Speakers)

### Options
**Option A: Virtual Visit**
- Invite a local helper to Zoom/Google Meet
- Prepare questions beforehand

**Option B: In-Person Visit**
- Fire truck visit
- Police officer presentation
- Healthcare worker talk

### Questions to Ask
1. What is your job?
2. How do you help people?
3. What training did you need?
4. What do you like best about your job?

---

## Lesson 3: How Helpers Work Together (45 min)

### Activity: Community Helper Chain

**Materials:** Strips of colored paper, markers, staplers

**Instructions:**
1. Each student writes/draws one way a helper serves the community
2. Connect strips to form a paper chain
3. Display around the classroom

**Discussion:**
- How do doctors and nurses work together?
- How do police and firefighters help each other?

---

## Lesson 4: Problem Solvers (45 min)

### Scenario Cards

Create scenarios where students must identify which helper can help:

| Scenario | Helper |
|----------|--------|
| Your house is on fire | Firefighter |
| Someone is sick | Doctor |
| You lost your dog | Police Officer |
| You need to learn to read | Teacher |
| Your food needs to grow | Farmer |

### Role Play Activity
Students act out helping scenarios and classmates guess the helper.

---

## Lesson 5: Thank You Project (45 min)

### Writing Activity
Students write thank-you letters to community helpers.

**Sentence Starters:**
- "Thank you for..."
- "I appreciate..."
- "You help our community by..."

### Class Book
Compile letters into a class book: "Thank You, Community Helpers!"

---

## Assessment Ideas

### Formative
- Observation during discussions
- Exit ticket: "Name 3 community helpers and what they do"

### Summative
- Student can correctly match 5+ helpers to their roles
- Student can explain why community helpers are important
- Completed thank you letter

---

## Family Connection

**Homework:** Interview a family member about their job or a job they'd like to have. Bring a photo or drawing to share.

**Family Letter:** Send home explaining the unit and suggesting community helper books at the library.

---

## Extensions & Differentiation

**For English Language Learners:**
- Use picture cards with words
- Partner with a peer helper

**For Advanced Learners:**
- Research additional helpers (veterinarian, plumber, electrician)
- Create a "Community Helper ABC book"

**For Students Needing Support:**
- Focus on 3-4 key helpers
- Provide visual scaffolds
