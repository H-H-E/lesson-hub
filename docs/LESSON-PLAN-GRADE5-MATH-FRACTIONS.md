# Lesson Plan: Fractions Are Friends - Grade 5 Math

## Overview

**Subject:** Mathematics - Fractions  
**Grade:** 5  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Class Size:** 20-30 students

---

## Curriculum Alignment

**BC Mathematics Curriculum Grade 5:**
- Number: Fractions - compare, order, and represent fractions
- Operations: Addition and subtraction of fractions with unlike denominators

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. **Identify** equivalent fractions using visual models
2. **Compare** fractions with unlike denominators
3. **Add** fractions with different denominators

---

## Materials Needed

- Fraction tiles/manipulatives (sets of 1, 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/6, 1/8)
- Whiteboards and markers
- Chart paper
- "Fraction Friends" worksheet
- Colored pencils
- Large fraction circle cutouts for demonstration

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Hook (5 min)

**Teacher says:** "Today we're going to meet some new friends - fractions! But these friends have a special secret: they can look different but still be equal. Can you keep a secret too?"

Show two different fraction circles that are the same size but divided differently.

**Think-Pair-Share:** "What do you notice?"

---

### Direct Instruction (15 min)

**Part 1: Equivalent Fractions**

1. Demonstrate using fraction tiles:
   - Show that 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6
   - Have students confirm with their manipulatives

2. Key rule on chart paper:
   > **Magic Rule:** Multiply or divide both top and bottom by the same number to find equivalent fractions!

3. Practice together:
   - 2/3 = ?/12 (multiply by 4)
   - 6/8 = 3/? (divide by 2)

---

### Guided Practice (15 min)

**Activity: "Fraction Friends Match"**

Students work in pairs to match equivalent fractions:

```
   1/2    =    2/4    =    3/6    =    4/8
   
   2/3    =    4/6    =    6/9
   
   3/4    =    6/8    =    9/12
```

**Challenge:** Students create their own matching sets on whiteboards.

---

### Independent Practice (15 min)

**Complete the "Fraction Friends" worksheet**

Students work individually on:
- Finding equivalent fractions
- Comparing fractions using >, <, or =
- Simple addition of fractions with like denominators

Teacher circulates to provide support and challenge as needed.

---

### Closure (10 min)

**Exit Ticket:** Quick check - students solve 3 problems on sticky notes:
1. Find an equivalent fraction for 3/5
2. Compare: 3/4 ? 5/8
3. Solve: 1/3 + 1/3 =

**Share:** Briefly discuss common misconceptions

---

## Differentiation

### For Students Who Need Support
- Provide fraction bars as visual scaffold
- Focus on halves, fourths, and eighths only
- Allow manipulatives during independent work
- Pre-teach key vocabulary

### For Advanced Learners
- Introduce mixed numbers
- Challenge: Subtract fractions with unlike denominators
- Create real-world word problems for classmates

---

## Assessment

**Formative:**
- Observation during pair work
- Whiteboard responses during guided practice
- Exit ticket analysis

**Summative:**
- Worksheet accuracy
- Unit test question performance

---

## Homework

**"Fraction Hunt"** - Find 3 examples of fractions in real life (recipes, measurements, sports stats). Draw and write about each one.

---

## Teacher Notes

**Common Misconceptions to Watch For:**
- "Bigger denominator = bigger fraction" - Address with visual models
- "Just add the numerators and denominators" - Review common denominator method

**Time Savers:**
- Pre-cut fraction tiles before class
- Use digital fraction manipulatives as backup
