# Grade 3 Curriculum - Code-First Inquiry

## BC Grade 3 Standards

### Science Big Ideas
- Living things are diverse, can be grouped, and interact in their environment
- Matter is useful because of its properties; properties determine use
- Energy can be transformed

### ADST
- Design can be for self, others, or the environment
- Skills are developed through design processes  
- Technologies extend human capabilities

---

## Module 1: Biodiversity 🌿

**Duration:** 45 minutes

### Learning Objectives
1. Classify local living things into groups
2. Describe biodiversity in an ecosystem
3. Explain how living things depend on each other

### Scaffolding
**Level 1:** Picture sorting cards, teacher-guided
**Level 2:** Partner classification task
**Level 3:** Create classification system for local area

### Assessment
- Exit ticket: Name 3 groups of living things
- Project: Biodiversity poster

---

## Module 2: Forces and Motion 🎢

**Duration:** 45 minutes

### Learning Objectives
1. Describe how forces affect motion
2. Test how different pushes and pulls work
3. Design something that uses forces

### The Mystery
> "How can you make something move without touching it?"

### Code Exploration
- Interactive simulation of forces
- Students add pushes/pulls
- Discover: Forces can be contact or at a distance

### Scaffolding
**Level 1:** Teacher demonstrates, students copy
**Level 2:** Guided experiments with recording sheet
**Level 3:** Design challenge

---

## Module 3: Energy Transformations ⚡

**Duration:** 45 minutes

### Learning Objectives
1. Identify forms of energy
2. Show how energy changes from one form to another
3. Investigate energy conservation

### The Mystery
> "Where does the energy go when you stop pushing?"

### Code Exploration
- Energy transformation simulation
- Track energy through a system
- Discover: Energy doesn't disappear, it changes form

---

## Module 4: Soil and Ecosystems 🌍

**Duration:** 45 minutes

### Learning Objectives
1. Describe the layers of soil
2. Explain how soil supports life
3. Investigate a local ecosystem

### First Peoples Integration
- Traditional knowledge of soil
- Indigenous farming practices
- Local ecosystem connections
