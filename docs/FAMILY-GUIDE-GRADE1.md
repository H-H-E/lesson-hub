# Family Guide - Code-First Inquiry

## What is Code-First Inquiry?

Your child is learning science through interactive simulations and coding. 
Instead of just reading about science, they EXPLORE and DISCOVER through hands-on activities.

---

## How to Support at Home

### Ask Questions
- "What did you discover today?"
- "What surprised you?"
- "What do you want to learn more about?"

### Explore Together
- Look for patterns in nature
- Notice science in everyday life
- Talk about how things work

### Resources
- p5js.org/editor - Free coding environment
- Try things at home with common materials

---

## Grade 1 Topics This Year

1. Living vs Non-Living
2. Matter (Solids, Liquids, Gases)
3. Light and Shadows
4. Patterns in the Sky (Moon, Sun)
5. Sound
6. Sorting and Classifying

---

## Questions to Ask Your Child

### After Module 1 (Living Things)
- What makes something "alive"?
- What are some living things in our home/garden?

### After Module 2 (Matter)
- Is water a solid or liquid?
- What happens when ice gets warm?

### After Module 3 (Light & Shadow)
- Where is your shadow in the morning? Afternoon?
- Can you make a shadow?

---

## At-Home Activities

### Living Things
- Go on a nature walk - find living vs non-living
- Watch birds - what do they do?

### Matter
- Freeze water and watch it melt
- Blow bubbles - is air a gas?

### Light & Shadow
- Flashlight shadow play
- Notice shadows at different times

### Sky Patterns
- Look at the moon each night
- Notice where the sun rises/sets

---

## Glossary

- **Living** - Things that grow, move, need food/water
- **Non-Living** - Things that were never alive
- **Solid** - Keeps its shape (rock, wood)
- **Liquid** - Flows, takes container shape (water)
- **Gas** - Spreads out (air, steam)
- **Pattern** - Something that repeats
- **Shadow** - When light is blocked
