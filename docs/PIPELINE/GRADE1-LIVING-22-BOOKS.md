# Recommended Books: Grade 1 Science - Living Things

## Picture Book Recommendations (Ages 6-7)

| # | Title | Author | Description |
|---|-------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **The Very Hungry Caterpillar** | Eric Carle | Classic story about metamorphosis - a caterpillar eats through various foods before transforming into a butterfly. Teaches life cycles and healthy eating. |
| 2 | **From Seed to Plant** | Gail Gibbons | Clear introduction to how plants grow, from seeds to roots, stems, and flowers. Includes labeled diagrams perfect for early readers. |
| 3 | **Are You a Snail?** | Judy Allen | Part of the "Backyard Books" series - explores the features and habitat of snails in an engaging, Q&A format for young scientists. |
| 4 | **The Growing Up Book of the Human Body** | Sophie Collins | Simple explanations of body systems - how we breathe, eat, and grow. Includes fun facts and colorful illustrations. |
| 5 | **I Spy Animals** | Jean Marzollo | Rhyming riddle book where children guess animals by looking at close-up photos. Great for building observation skills. |
| 6 | **Bug Club: Minibeast Magic** | Various | Collection of simple books about insects, worms, and other small creatures found in gardens. Controlled vocabulary for early readers. |

---
*Note: This list supports the Grade 1 "Living Things" science unit covering plants, animals, and human growth. Books are selected for readability, engagement, and curriculum alignment.*
