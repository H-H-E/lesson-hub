# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Explorer's Journal Prompts

## Overview

These Explorer's Journal prompts support Grade 1 students in reflecting on their learning about living things. Prompts are organized into **Before**, **During**, and **After** sections to promote metacognition and deepen understanding.

---

## BEFORE: Activating Prior Knowledge

### Prompt 1: What Is Living?
> **Draw or write:** What do you think makes something alive? What do living things have that non-living things don't?

*Sample student response:* "Living things move and grow. My dog moves and I grow."

### Prompt 2: My Living Things
> **Draw or write:** Name 3 living things you see every day. Where do you see them?

*Sample student response:* "I see a dog, a tree, and flowers in my yard."

### Prompt 3: How Have You Changed?
> **Draw or write:** How have you changed since you were a baby? What is different now?

*Sample student response:* "I am taller. I can read books. I have more teeth."

---

## DURING: During Learning Activities

### Prompt 4: Living or Not Living?
> **Sort and explain:** Look at the pictures. Which are living and which are not? Why did you sort them that way?

*Activity tip:* Students sort picture cards and write/draw their reasoning.

### Prompt 5: What Do Living Things Need?
> **Draw and label:** Draw a living thing (animal or plant). Show what it needs to stay alive.

*Activity tip:* Include food, water, air, and sunlight for plants.

### Prompt 6: Life Cycle Observation
> **Observe and draw:** Watch the bean seed grow. Draw what you see each day. What changes?

*Activity tip:* Include space for 3-4 drawing frames over 2 weeks.

### Prompt 7: Animal Investigation
> **Research and illustrate:** Choose one animal. Draw it. What does it eat? Where does it live? How does it grow?

*Activity tip:* Partner with library time for animal research.

### Prompt 8: Plant Parts
> **Label and explore:** Draw a plant. Label the roots, stem, leaves, and flowers. What does each part do?

*Activity tip:* Use real plants from the garden for hands-on exploration.

---

## AFTER: Reflecting on Learning

### Prompt 9: Living Things Hunt
> **Reflect:** Where can you find living things? Make a list or draw pictures of living things in your neighborhood.

*Sample student response:* "Birds in trees, bugs in the grass, flowers in the garden."

### Prompt 10: How Are We Alike?
> **Compare:** How are humans and animals the same? How are plants and animals different?

*Activity tip:* Use a simple Venn diagram or T-chart.

### Prompt 11: My Favorite Living Thing
> **Celebrate learning:** What is your favorite living thing? Why? Draw it and tell why you chose it.

*Extension:* Create a mini-poster for classroom display.

### Prompt 12: New Learning
> **Reflect:** What is something NEW you learned about living things that you didn't know before?

*Sample student response:* "I didn't know plants need air too!"

### Prompt 13: Take Care of Living Things
> **Connect to action:** How can we help take care of living things? Name 3 things we can do.

*Sample student response:* "Plant trees, don't litter, feed birds."

---

## Journal Format Options

- **Draw-First Approach:** Large drawing space with sentence starters for emerging writers
- **Labeling Practice:** Include word banks for key vocabulary
- **Photo Documentation:** Allow students to glue in photos of real observations
- **Partner Dictation:** Students share orally while teacher or volunteer records

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For students needing support:** Provide word banks, picture prompts, and sentence frames
- **For advanced learners:** Add "I wonder..." questions to extend thinking
- **For English Language Learners:** Include visual vocabulary cards with each prompt
