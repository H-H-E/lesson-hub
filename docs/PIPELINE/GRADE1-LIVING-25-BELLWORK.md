# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Bell Work / Warm-Up Activities

---

## Overview

**Purpose:** Quick, engaging activities (2-5 minutes) to start the lesson and activate prior knowledge about living things.

**When to Use:** Beginning of class, during transitions, or as quick formative assessments.

---

## 🌅 Morning Warm-Up: "Is It Alive?" Quick Sort

### Day 1: Introduction (3 minutes)

**Setup:** Display images or hold up real objects (or use whiteboard drawings)

**Teacher says:**
> "Good morning, scientists! Let's start with a quick challenge. I'm going to show you some things — you tell me: alive or not alive? Thumbs up if you think it's alive, thumbs down if not alive!"

**Items to show (one at a time):**
- 🐕 A dog photo → Thumbs up (alive!)
- 🪨 A rock → Thumbs down (not alive)
- 🌻 A sunflower → Thumbs up (alive!)
- 🚗 A car → Thumbs down (not alive)
- 🐱 A cat → Thumbs up (alive!)
- 🧸 A stuffed teddy → Thumbs down (not alive!)

**Quick discussion:**
> "Interesting! Some of you had different answers for the teddy. That's okay — we'll figure out the rules together!"

---

### Day 2: "What Do Living Things Have?" (3 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
> "Yesterday we sorted things. Today, let's think: WHAT makes something alive? I need 3 volunteers!"

**Call up 3 students:**
- One to pretend to eat (take a bite of "apple")
- One to pretend to breathe (deep breath)
- One to pretend to grow (start small, then stretch up tall)

**Teacher says:**
> "Living things eat! Living things breathe! Living things grow! What else do living things do?" *(Pause for ideas: move, need water, have babies)*

---

## 🔄 Transition Activity: "Living or Not?"

### Quick Partner Game (2 minutes)

**Instructions:**
> "Turn to your neighbor! I'll say something — you and your partner decide together: alive or not alive? You have 10 seconds to decide!"

**Call out (rapidly):**
1. "A tree!" → Alive
2. "A pencil!" → Not alive
3. "A baby!" → Alive
4. "A cloud!" → Not alive (discuss: moves but doesn't eat/grow)
5. "A fish!" → Alive
6. "Ice cream!" → Not alive

---

## 🧠 Thinking Prompt: "What Am I?"

### Day 3: Mystery Riddles (4 minutes)

**Teacher says:**
> "I'm thinking of something. Listen to the clues!"

**Riddle 1:**
> "I have leaves. I grow tall. I make food from the sun. What am I?" → *(Allow guesses)* → **A plant!**

**Riddle 2:**
> "I have legs. I can walk. I eat food. I grow bigger. What am I?" → *(Allow guesses)* → **An animal!**

**Riddle 3:**
> "I move but I don't eat. I don't grow. I was made in a factory. What am I?" → *(Allow guesses)* → **A toy!**

**Wrap-up:**
> "Plants and animals are living. Toys are NOT living. You're becoming great detectives!"

---

## 🎵 Song & Movement Warm-Up

### "The Living Things Song" (2 minutes)

**Sung to a simple tune (like "Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes"):**

> *"Living things move, living things grow,*  
> *Living things breathe — yes, we know!*  
> *Plants and animals, every one,*  
> *All living things need the sun!"*

**Actions:**
- "Move" → wave arms
- "Grow" → stretch up tall
- "Breathe" → put hand on chest
- "Sun" → make a big circle with arms

---

## ✏️ Quick Draw & Label (Assessment for Learning)

### Day 4: "Draw Something Living" (3-4 minutes)

**Instructions:**
> "On your paper, draw ONE living thing. It can be anything — an animal, a plant, even YOU! When you're done, label it or tell me one thing it does that shows it's alive."

**Circulate and ask:**
- "What did you draw?"
- "How do you know it's alive?"
- "What does it need to stay alive?"

---

## 🌿 Nature Observation (If Available)

### Day 5: "Look Out the Window" (2 minutes)

**If you have a window or outdoor view:**

> "Look outside! Find something living. Point to it! What is it doing that's alive?"

**Possible observations:**
- Birds (flying, sitting)
- Trees/leaves (moving in wind)
- Clouds (moving — discuss: is this living? Not really — they move but don't eat or grow!)
- Bugs

**Follow-up:**
> "What about that bench? Is it alive? What about the grass? YES! Grass is living — it grows!"

## 🎯 Bell Work Teacher Checklist

| Time | Activity | Focus Skill |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Day 1 | Sort pictures | Identify living vs. non-living |
| Day 2 | Actions + discussion | Understand what living things do |
| Day 3 | Riddles | Apply knowledge |
| Day 4 | Draw & label | Create / explain |
| Day 5 | Observation | Connect to real world |

---

## 💡 Tips for Success

- **Keep it fast** — don't let any activity drag beyond 4-5 minutes
- **Accept all guesses** — this is about building thinking, not getting "right" answers
- **Celebrate mistakes** — wrong guesses are great learning opportunities
- **Connect to the hook** — reference "Mr. Wiggles" and "Ms. Green" if you used them
- **Use wait time** — give 3-5 seconds after asking "Is it alive?" before calling on students

---

## 🔄 Differentiation Options

**For students needing support:**
- Provide fewer choices (A or B)
- Use real objects instead of pictures
- Offer sentence stems: "I think ____ is alive because..."

**For advanced students:**
- Ask: "Could something be both living and not living?" (seeds, spores)
- Challenge: "What about fire? Is fire alive? Why or why not?"

---

*Bell work activities should be ready to go in under 1 minute. Keep materials simple and accessible!*
