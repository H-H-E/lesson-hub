# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Assessment Section

## Overview

This assessment section provides a comprehensive evaluation plan for the Grade 1 Living Things unit. It includes formative assessments for ongoing monitoring, a summative project for culminating demonstration of learning, quiz questions for knowledge checks, and a detailed rubric for consistent scoring.

---

## Formative Assessments

### 1. Observation Checklist (Ongoing)

**Purpose:** Monitor student understanding during hands-on activities and class discussions.

**Instructions:** Teacher circles the descriptor that matches student performance during each activity.

| Criterion | Developing (1) | Approaching (2) | Proficient (3) | Exemplary (4) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Identifies Living Things** | Identifies only obvious living things (pets, people) | Identifies living things with prompting | Independently identifies 5+ living things | Identifies living things and explains why they are living |
| **Uses Characteristics** | Cannot yet use characteristics to identify living things | Names 1 characteristic with prompting | Names 2-3 characteristics correctly | Applies characteristics to new examples confidently |
| **Participates in Discussions** | Rarely speaks or responds | Responds when called on | Offers ideas voluntarily | Builds on peers' contributions with relevant comments |
| **Records Observations** | Needs significant help to draw or write | Draws with teacher guidance | Independently records observations | Creates detailed, labeled drawings with descriptions |

**Total Points Possible:** 16 points

### 2. Exit Ticket (End of Lesson Check)

**Instructions:** Students complete this quick check at the end of each major lesson. Teachers can choose one or use all three throughout the unit.

#### Exit Ticket A: Living or Not Living?

**Circle the answer:**

1. Is a tree living or not living?
   - Living  /  Not Living

2. Is a rock living or not living?
   - Living  /  Not Living

3. Is a dog living or not living?
   - Living  /  Not Living

4. Is a teddy bear living or not living?
   - Living  /  Not Living

5. Is grass living or not living?
   - Living  /  Not Living

**Scoring:** 5 points total (1 point each)

---

#### Exit Ticket B: What Do Living Things Need?

**Draw a line to match:**

```
🐟 Fish       →     Food
🌱 Plant      →     Water
👶 Baby       →     Air
```

**Complete the sentence:**
Living things need _____________ to survive.

**Scoring:** 4 points total (1 point each match, 1 point for sentence)

---

#### Exit Ticket C: How Do Living Things Grow?

**Check (✓) the things that are true:**

- [ ] Living things get bigger as they grow
- [ ] Dogs stay the same size forever
- [ ] Baby chicks become chickens
- [ ] Rocks change and grow

**Draw one way a living thing changes as it grows:**
____________________________________________

**Scoring:** 5 points total (3 points for checkboxes, 2 points for drawing)

## Summative Assessment

### Project: "My Living Thing Friend"

**Project Description:** Each student selects and investigates one living thing (an animal or plant) and creates a poster to teach the class about it.

**Project Requirements:**

1. **Choose Your Living Thing:** Select an animal or plant you want to learn about (approved by teacher)

2. **Research (with teacher support):**
   - What does it look like?
   - Where does it live?
   - What does it eat?
   - How does it grow?

3. **Create Your Poster:**
   - Draw or print a picture of your living thing
   - Label 3 body parts (if animal) or 3 parts (if plant)
   - Write 3 facts about your living thing
   - Answer: Why is it a living thing?

4. **Present:** Share your poster with the class (2-3 minutes)

**Materials Provided:**
- Large construction paper
- Markers, crayons, colored pencils
- Printed images (if available)
- Sentence starters and word banks

**Time:** 2 class periods for creation + 1 period for presentations

## Quiz Questions

### Quiz 1: Living and Non-Living

**Circle the correct answer:**

1. Which one is a living thing?
   - A) Rock
   - B) Tree
   - C) Car
   - D) Chair

2. Which one is NOT living?
   - A) Dog
   - B) Flower
   - C) Water
   - D) Cat

3. Living things can:
   - A) Only stay still
   - B) Move by themselves
   - C) Never grow
   - D) Make their own food (trick question!)

4. A baby kitten grows into an adult cat. This is an example of:
   - A) Sleeping
   - B) Growing and changing
   - C) Playing
   - D) Eating

**Scoring:** 4 points (1 point each)

---

### Quiz 2: Needs of Living Things

**Answer each question:**

1. Name 2 things all living things need to survive.
   _______________________________________________

2. Why do living things need food? (Draw or write)
   _______________________________________________

3. Draw what happens to a plant when it gets water vs. no water.

   [Space for drawing]

4. A fish is in a bowl with no water. What will happen?
   _______________________________________________

**Scoring:** 8 points total

---

### Quiz 3: Animals vs. Plants

**Match the column:**

Column A | Column B
---------|----------
1. Animals | a. Make their own food
2. Plants | b. Eat other things
3. What animals need | c. Have roots, stem, leaves
4. What plants need | d. Food, water, air, shelter
   | e. Move around

**Answers:** 1-b, 2-a/c, 3-d, 4-c/d

**Scoring:** 4 points

## Rubric: "My Living Thing Friend" Project

| Criterion | 1 - Beginning | 2 - Developing | 3 - Proficient | 4 - Exemplary | Score |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|----------------|-------|
| **Content Accuracy** | Shows limited understanding of the living thing | Shows some understanding but with errors | Accurately describes the living thing with 3 facts | Provides detailed, accurate information with 5+ facts | /4 |
| **Visual Representation** | Drawing is unclear or missing | Drawing is present but lacks detail | Clear drawing with 3 labeled parts | Detailed, colorful drawing with 5+ labels and annotations | /4 |
| **Understanding of "Living"** | Cannot explain why it is living | Explains living with 1 characteristic | Explains living using 2-3 characteristics | Connects characteristics to all aspects of the project | /4 |
| **Presentation** | Does not present or cannot explain project | Presents with significant teacher support | Presents clearly with minimal support | Engaging presentation, answers questions confidently | /4 |
| **Effort & Neatness** | Incomplete or messy | Some effort shown, some messiness | Well-done and organized | Extra effort shown, creative and polished | /4 |

**Total Score:** /20

## Test Security Note

All quiz questions and exit tickets should be administered under teacher supervision. Exit tickets may be re-administered if students need additional opportunities to demonstrate understanding.

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For students needing support:** Provide sentence starters, word banks, and one-on-one scaffolding during activities
- **For advanced students:** Encourage additional research, complex drawings, or adding interactive elements to posters
- **For English Language Learners:** Allow verbal responses, use visuals, and provide bilingual vocabulary lists
