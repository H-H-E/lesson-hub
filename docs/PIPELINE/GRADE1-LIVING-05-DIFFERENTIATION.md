# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Differentiation Guide

## Overview

This document provides tiered activities to support all learners in achieving the Grade 1 Living Things objectives:
- Identify 5+ living things with 80% accuracy
- Describe 3 ways living things grow and change
- Distinguish living vs non-living using 3 characteristics
- Explain why living things need food, water, and air

---

## Level 1: Highest Support 🟢

*For students who need more scaffolding, visual supports, and guided practice*

### Learning Activities

**Activity 1: Living Things Picture Sort**
- Provide pre-sorted cards with pictures and labels
- Use real photographs (not drawings) for clarity
- Include only 6-8 items total (3 living, 3 non-living)
- Teacher models sorting first with think-aloud
- Matching worksheet with traceable words

**Activity 2: Growth Story Cards**
- Use sequence cards showing one animal's growth (e.g., puppy → dog)
- Students arrange in order with teacher guidance
- Verbal responses accepted instead of written
- Visual cue cards: "grows," "changes," "needs"

**Activity 3: Living vs. Non-Living Picture Walk**
- Whole-class activity with large images
- Teacher points and asks: "Does it move on its own?" (yes/no cards)
- Partner work with teacher nearby
- Fill-in-the-blank sentences: "A dog is _______ (living/not living)"

**Activity 4: Basic Needs Mini-Book**
- Pre-cut pages with pictures already glued
- Students trace: "All living things need ______"
- Picture prompts for food, water, air, shelter
- One focus question per page

### Assessment Modifications
- Oral responses accepted
- Point to answer format
- Extra time (1.5x)
- One-on-one or small group assessment

## Level 2: Guided Practice 🟡

*For students who need moderate support with some independent practice*

### Learning Activities

**Activity 1: Living Things Hunt**
- Student-friendly checklist with 10 items (pictures + words)
- Go on classroom/school hunt for living things
- Record findings by circling or checking off items
- Add 2-3 of their own discoveries
- Sort worksheet: Living / Not Living (10 items)

**Activity 2: How Have I Grown?**
- Personal growth chart (baby photo → now)
- Compare with a plant's growth (plant in cup over 2 weeks)
- Venn diagram: "How I'm like a plant" (with teacher help)
- Write or draw 2 ways living things grow

**Activity 3: Three Clues Game**
- Teacher describes: "I move on my own, I need food, I grow. What am I?"
- Students hold up picture cards to answer
- Partner game: Students take turns giving clues
- Independent worksheet: Sort 12 items, explain 1 reason

**Activity 4: Why Do Living Things Need...?**
- Sort activity: What do humans need? What do plants need?
- Anchor chart: "All Living Things Need..." (partially filled)
- Write or draw 2 things every living thing needs
- Compare: How are your needs similar to a pet's?

### Assessment Modifications
- Written or oral responses
- Sentence starters provided
- Standard time
- Small group assessment option

## Level 3: Extension 🔴

*For students ready for more challenge and independent work*

### Learning Activities

**Activity 1: Living Things Expert Card**
- Create a "profile card" for a living thing
- Must include: name, habitat, food, 2 ways it grows/changes
- Research (with books/prints): Find 3 new living things
- Present to class: "Amazing Living Thing" spotlight

**Activity 2: Life Cycle Comparison**
- Compare two life cycles (butterfly vs. frog, or dog vs. cat)
- Create a poster showing both with labels
- Answer: "How are these growth patterns the same? Different?"
- Write 3 sentences about growth and change

**Activity 3: Mystery Object Challenge**
- Given 5 mysterious items (rock, seed, toy car, leaf, apple)
- Students determine: Living or not living? Why?
- Must use all 3 characteristics (movement, food/air, growth)
- Write explanations with evidence
- Peer teaching: Explain to a classmate

**Activity 4: Design a Creature**
- Create an imaginary living thing
- Must meet ALL basic needs: food, water, air, shelter
- Draw and label: What does it eat? Where does it live? How does it grow?
- Write 3 facts about your creature
- "Would it survive in our school? Why or why not?"

### Assessment Modifications
- Written explanations expected
- Challenge questions included
- Extension rubrics
- Self-assessment checklist

## Differentiation Strategies by Objective

| Objective | Level 1 Support | Level 2 Support | Level 3 Extension |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Identify 5+ living things | Picture matching with labels | Picture hunt with checklist | Research new living things |
| Describe 3 growth changes | Sequence cards, verbal | Personal comparison, drawing | Compare two life cycles |
| Distinguish living/non-living | Teacher-guided sort, yes/no | Independent sort, 1 reason | All 3 reasons + mystery objects |
| Explain basic needs | Trace sentences, picture prompts | Sort and compare | Design a creature with needs |
