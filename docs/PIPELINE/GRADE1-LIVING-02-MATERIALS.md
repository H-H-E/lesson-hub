## 📋 Preparation Timeline

### 2-3 Days Before Lesson

- [ ] Prepare "Living vs. Non-Living" picture cards (10 living, 10 non-living)
- [ ] Plant bean seeds in clear plastic cups (need 3-4 days for visible roots)
- [ ] Gather collection samples
- [ ] Set up outdoor space (confirm safety, boundaries)

### Day Before Lesson

- [ ] Print "Living Chain" worksheet templates
- [ ] Prepare Explorer's Journal pages (or have students create them)
- [ ] Set out mystery hook items under cloth

### Day Of Lesson

- [ ] Check timer/bell availability
- [ ] Verify outdoor space access
- [ ] Test p5.js simulation on projector (if using)
- [ ] Organize magnifying glasses

## 💰 Budget Considerations

Most items are low-cost or reusable:

- **Free/Recycled:** Picture cards, rock, pine cone, photographs
- **Low-Cost ($5-15):** Sticky notes, magnifying glasses, bean seeds
- **One-Time Purchase:** Chart paper, markers, crayons (lasts multiple units)
- **Optional Investment:** Pre-made "Living vs. Non-Living" card sets available online

---

## 📝 Teacher Notes

- Magnifying glasses can be shared (5-8 for ~25 students)
- If outdoor walk isn't possible, bring items inside or use windowsill plants
- Bean seeds should show visible roots for the mystery hook
- Colored pencils are optional—crayons work well for Grade 1
