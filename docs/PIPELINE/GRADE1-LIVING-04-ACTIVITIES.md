# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Lesson Activities

**Lesson 4: What Do Living Things Need?**

---

## 📋 Lesson Overview
- **Grade Level:** 1
- **Topic:** Living Things
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Learning Goal:** Students will understand that living things need food, water, air, and shelter to survive.

---

## Phase 1: Explore (10 minutes)

### 🔍 Activity: "What Do I Need?"

**Timing:** 10 min (Introduction: 3 min | Activity: 7 min)

**Materials:**
- Picture cards of a child, a dog, a tree, a fish
- Chart paper with headings: "Food" "Water" "Air" "Home"

**Instructions:**
1. **Show** the four picture cards and ask: "Which of these are alive?"
2. **Discuss** briefly what makes something "living" (moves, grows, needs things)
3. **Hand out** one card to each table group
4. **Guide** students to sort their card under the correct need category on the chart

**Differentiation:**
- *Scaffold:* Provide a completed example before they begin
- *Extend:* Ask "What would happen if the fish had no water?"

**Teacher Notes:** Focus on prior knowledge. Most first graders know animals need food/water but may not connect plants to these needs.

---

## Phase 2: Discover (12 minutes)

### 🧪 Activity: "Growing Seeds Experiment"

**Timing:** 12 min (Demonstration: 3 min | Observation: 9 min)

**Materials:**
- 3 clear cups with cotton balls
- Bean seeds (pre-soaked)
- Water
- Plastic wrap

**Setup (before class):**
- Cup 1: Seeds + wet cotton (normal)
- Cup 2: Seeds + dry cotton (no water)
- Cup 3: Seeds + wet cotton + placed in dark closet

**Instructions:**
1. **Show** all three cups side by side
2. **Ask:** "What do you think will happen to each cup?"
3. **Have students** observe and record on worksheet (draw what they see)
4. **Predict:** Which cup will grow? Which won't? Why?

**Questions to Ask:**
- "What does the seed in Cup 2 need that it doesn't have?"
- "Why might Cup 3 grow differently than Cup 1?"

**Safety Note:** Remind students not to eat the seeds.

---

## Phase 3: Create (15 minutes)

### 🎨 Activity: "My Pet's Care Guide"

**Timing:** 15 min (Instructions: 3 min | Work time: 10 min | Sharing: 2 min)

**Materials:**
- Paper folded into "book" format (4 pages)
- Crayons, markers
- Picture stickers (optional)
- Sample care guide (for display)

**Instructions:**
1. **Show** your sample care guide (for a pet goldfish or classroom plant)
2. **Explain** the four things all living things need
3. **Students create** their own 4-page book:
   - Page 1: Draw their pet or chosen living thing
   - Page 2: Draw what food it eats
   - Page 3: Draw where it lives/shelter
   - Page 4: Draw it with water and air

**Differentiation:**
- *Scaffold:* Provide sentence starters ("My pet needs...")
- *Extend:* Add a page about what happens if it doesn't get these things

**Assessment Opportunity:** Check that students include all 4 basic needs in their books.

---

## Phase 4: Check (8 minutes)

### ✅ Activity: "Living Things Check-Up"

**Timing:** 8 min (Individual: 5 min | Discussion: 3 min)

**Materials:**
- Exit ticket worksheet (half-sheet)
- Pencil

**Exit Ticket (3 questions):**

1. **Circle the living thing:**
   - 🪨 Rock / 🐟 Fish / 🌙 Moon

2. **Draw two things a plant needs to live:**
   - [blank space for drawing]

3. **True or False:**
   - A dog can live without water. (True / False)

**Alternative (if time permits):** Whole-class "Thumbs Up/Down" game
- Teacher reads statements → students show thumbs up (true) or down (false)

**Success Criteria:**
- 2/3 correct = Mastery
- Review misconceptions in next lesson
