# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Lesson Closure & Wrap-Up

## 🌿 Closing Activity Overview

**Lesson Focus:** What Do Living Things Need?
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes
**Purpose:** Reinforce learning, check understanding, and create a satisfying lesson end

---

## 🎯 Quick Closure Activities (5 min)

### 1. "Living Things Review" - Thumb Cards
**Instructions:**
- Students hold up thumbs up (✅) or thumbs down (❌)
- Teacher reads statements; students respond:

| Statement | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "A rock is a living thing." | ❌ |
| "Plants need water to live." | ✅ |
| "Animals need food and air." | ✅ |
| "A toy car can grow." | ❌ |
| "Fish need a home to live in." | ✅ |

**Purpose:** Quick formative assessment; identify misconceptions

---

### 2. "One Thing I Learned" - Pair Share
**Instructions:**
1. Students turn to a partner
2. Each student shares ONE thing they learned today
3. Partner listens and nods
4. Switch roles (30 seconds each)

**Sentence Starters (display on board):**
- "I learned that living things need..."
- "I was surprised that..."
- "Now I know why..."

**Purpose:** Oral processing; peer reinforcement

---

## 🧩 Standard Closure Activities (10 min)

### 3. "Living Things EXIT Tickets" - Quick Check

**Question 1:** Draw one thing YOU need to stay alive and healthy.

*(Space for student drawing)*

**Question 2:** Circle the living thing:
- 🪨 Rock
- 🐕 Dog  
- 🌙 Moon

**Question 3:** True or False?
- "A plant can live without any water." (False)

**Success Criteria:**
- 2/3 correct = Lesson mastery achieved
- Review any wrong answers as a class

---

### 4. "My Learning Anchor" - Take-Home Summary

**Instructions:**
- Each student receives a "Living Things" bookmark
- Students color and complete the sentence:
  - *"Living things need __________, __________, __________, and __________."*

**Take-Home:** Students take their Care Guide Book and bookmark home to share with families

---

## 🎉 Enhanced Closure Activities (15 min)

### 5. "Living Things Museum" - Gallery Walk

**Setup:**
- Display student Care Guide Books on desks
- Walk around the room (2 min)
- Each student places a sticky dot on their favorite book

**Instructions:**
1. "Walk like a museum visitor"
2. Look at friends' work
3. Place one dot on a book you like

**Debrief:**
- "What did you notice about everyone's books?"
- "Did anyone include something surprising?"

---

### 6. "Growth Chart" - Looking Forward

**Instructions:**
1. Draw a simple picture of yourself on the "Today" side of a chart
2. Predict: "What will I look like when I'm grown up?"
3. Draw a quick picture on the "Grown Up" side
4. Discuss: "What changes will happen as I grow?"

**Connection:** Connect to how all living things grow and change

---

## 📚 Home Connection (Optional Extension)

### "Living Things Hunt" - Family Activity

**Send home:**
- "Living Things Hunt" worksheet

**Instructions for families:**
- Go for a walk around your neighborhood
- Find and count:
  - 3 living things that are animals
  - 3 living things that are plants
  - 2 non-living things
- Discuss: "What do they all need to stay alive?"

## 📝 Teacher Notes

**Key Points to Reinforce:**
1. Living things need food, water, air, and shelter
2. Plants are living things too!
3. We can observe and experiment to learn about living things

**Common Misconceptions to Address:**
- "Rocks are alive because they move" (no—they move by forces, not themselves)
- "Toys are alive" (remind: toys don't eat, grow, or breathe)
- "Plants don't need water" (show the seed experiment results)

**Celebration:** Praise students for great observation and hard work!
