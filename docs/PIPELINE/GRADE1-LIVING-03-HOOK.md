# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Hook (2-3 minutes)

## The Mystery of the Moving Rock

---

### 🎬 Hook Opening (30 seconds)

> **Teacher says:**
> "Class, I have something VERY special in my hand today. I need everyone to sit super still and use your super-scientist eyes."

*(Hold up a small box or covered container)*

> "Inside this box... is something that moves! But wait — is it alive? Let's find out!"

---

### 🔍 The Mystery (1 minute)

> "I want you to meet my friend... **Mr. Wiggles**!"

*(Pull out a toy that moves — wind-up toy, remote-controlled item, or even a simple spinning top)*

> "Look! Mr. Wiggles moves all by himself! He goes round and round! Is Mr. Wiggles ALIVE?"

*(Pause — let students call out answers)*

> "Hmm... he moves, but does he eat? Does he breathe? Does he grow?"

*(Make a thinking face)*

> "Let's meet another friend..."

*(Pull out a potted plant OR show a picture)*

> "This is **Ms. Green**. She doesn't move at all. She just sits there. Is Ms. Green ALIVE?"

*(Pause for responses)*

---

### ❓ The Big Question (30 seconds)

> "Here's the mystery, scientists: 
> - Mr. Wiggles MOVES but doesn't eat or grow... 
> - Ms. Green DOESN'T move but she DOES eat and grow!
> 
> Which one is alive? How can we tell?"

> "This week, we're going to become **Living Thing Detectives**! We'll learn the secret clues that tell us if something is alive or NOT alive."

---

### ✨ Preview (15 seconds)

> "By the end of our lessons, you'll be able to look at ANYTHING — a rock, a dog, a toy, a tree — and know: Is it living or NOT living? You'll have SUPERPOWERS!"

> "Let's begin! First question: What makes something ALIVE?"

---

## 🎯 Hook Learning Goals

- **Spark curiosity** about what makes something "alive"
- **Create wonder** through the mystery of the moving vs. non-moving objects
- **Set purpose** — students become "Living Thing Detectives"
- **Hook into prior knowledge** — get them thinking about their own experiences with living things

## 🛠️ Materials Needed

- A box to hide items
- One "moving" object that isn't alive (toy, spinning top, wind-up toy)
- One living plant OR picture of a plant
- Optional: simple puppet or character to give the objects personality

## 💡 Teacher Notes

- **Keep it fun** — don't give away the answer yet! Let them wonder.
- **Accept all answers** — if students say the toy is alive, that's okay! That's what we're going to figure out.
- **Use expression** — be dramatic! This is the "mystery" part.
- **Connect to their world** — you might ask: "Is your stuffed animal alive?" (Tricky question that gets them thinking!)

---

*Duration: ~2 minutes 45 seconds*
