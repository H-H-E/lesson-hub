# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Extension Activities

**Extension Projects for Fast Finishers & Deep Learners**

---

## 📋 Extension Overview
- **Grade Level:** 1
- **Topic:** Living Things
- **Purpose:** Provide enrichment activities for students who complete the main lesson early or want to explore living things in greater depth
- **Duration:** 20-30 minutes per project (can be completed over multiple sessions)

---

## 🌱 Extension Project 1: "Living Things Detective"

### 🔍 Project Description
Students become "Living Things Detectives" and go on a nature walk (or look through books/magazines) to find and document living things. They create a "detective notebook" with their findings.

### 🎯 Learning Goals
- Apply knowledge of what makes something "living"
- Practice observation and recording skills
- Distinguish between living and non-living things

### 📦 Materials
- Detective notebook (any blank booklet or stapled paper)
- Crayons or markers
- Magnifying glass (optional)
- Pencils

### 📝 Activity Steps
1. **Create your detective badge** - Decorate the cover of your notebook with "Living Things Detective"
2. **Go on a hunt** - Walk around the schoolyard, playground, or use picture books to find living things
3. **Record findings** - For each living thing found, draw a picture and write/or tell:
   - What is it?
   - What does it need to survive?
   - How do you know it's alive?
4. **Create a "Wanted" list** - Make a page showing living things they'd like to find more of

### ✅ Success Criteria
- [ ] Found and recorded at least 5 living things
- [ ] Included what each living thing needs
- [ ] Explained why each is a living thing

### 🔄 Differentiation
- *Scaffold:* Provide a template with prompts ("This is a ______. It needs ______.")
- *Extend:* Add a "non-living things" comparison page

## 🐛 Extension Project 2: "Life Cycle Storybook"

### 📖 Project Description
Students create an illustrated storybook showing the life cycle of a living thing (butterfly, frog, chicken, or plant).

### 🎯 Learning Goals
- Understand that living things grow and change
- Practice sequential thinking and storytelling
- Develop fine motor skills through drawing

### 📦 Materials
- 4-6 sheets of paper stapled together
- crayons, colored pencils
- Pencil
- Sample life cycle image (optional)

### 📝 Activity Steps
1. **Choose your living thing** - Pick from: butterfly, frog, chicken, or bean plant
2. **Plan your pages** - Decide on 4 stages:
   - Baby stage
   - Growing stage
   - Adult stage
   - Making new babies (cycle continues)
3. **Draw and write** - On each page:
   - Draw a picture of that stage
   - Write or dictate what is happening ("The caterpillar eats leaves.")
4. **Add a cover** - Title your book and decorate the front

### ✅ Success Criteria
- [ ] Shows at least 4 stages of growth
- [ ] Pictures clearly show each stage
- [ ] Includes labels or sentences for each stage

### 🔄 Differentiation
- *Scaffold:* Provide a sequence template with pictures to color
- *Extend:* Create a "Compare and Contrast" page showing two different life cycles

## 🌿 Extension Project 3: "Living Thing Care Kit"

### 🧰 Project Description
Students design a "care kit" for a living thing of their choice, showing everything it needs to survive. This could be for a pet, a plant, or an imaginary creature!

### 🎯 Learning Goals
- Apply understanding of survival needs (food, water, air, shelter)
- Practice creative problem-solving
- Demonstrate empathy and responsibility

### 📦 Materials
- Large paper plate or cardboard circle
- Markers, crayons
- Small items to glue (optional): cotton balls, paper scraps, ribbon
- Scissors
- Glue stick

### 📝 Activity Steps
1. **Choose your living thing** - Select an animal, plant, or create an imaginary creature
2. **Design your care kit** - On the plate, create sections showing:
   - Food section: Draw or include what food your creature needs
   - Water section: Show how it gets water
   - Air section: Where does it breathe? What kind of air does it need?
   - Home section: Draw its shelter/habitat
3. **Label each section** - Write words like "Food," "Water," "Air," "Home"
4. **Add a "Care Guide"** - On the back, write or tell 3 rules for taking care of this living thing

### ✅ Success Criteria
- [ ] Includes all 4 basic needs (food, water, air, home)
- [ ] Clearly shows what their specific living thing needs
- [ ] Has labeled sections
- [ ] Care guide shows understanding of responsibility

### 🔄 Differentiation
- *Scaffold:* Provide a pre-divided plate template with labels
- *Extend:* Add a "What would happen if..." page (e.g., "What if it had no water?")

## 🎒 Teacher Notes

### When to Offer Extensions
- During independent work time after completing main activities
- As early finisher choices on a menu
- As homework enrichment options
- During center time rotations

### Tips for Managing Extensions
- Prepare materials in advance in "extension bags"
- Have students store work in a "finished work" folder
- Allow students to choose based on interest
- Celebrate completed extensions with a "Science Showcase"

### Assessment of Extensions
- Review completed work against success criteria
- Use for formative assessment of understanding
- Share with class (with permission) to inspire others
- Add to student portfolio

These extensions reinforce:
- ✅ Living things have needs (food, water, air, shelter)
- ✅ Living things grow and change
- ✅ Living things can be found everywhere
- ✅ We care for living things
