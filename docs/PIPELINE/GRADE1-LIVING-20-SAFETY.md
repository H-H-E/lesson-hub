# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Safety Considerations

**Lesson 20: Safety Guidelines**

---

## Overview

This document outlines safety protocols for the Grade 1 "Living Things" lesson. All activities involve non-hazardous materials appropriate for early primary students, but proper supervision and proactive safety measures ensure a safe learning environment.

---

## 🧪 Activity-Specific Safety Notes

### Growing Seeds Experiment

| Hazard | Risk Level | Mitigation |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Small seeds (choking) | Low-Medium | Supervise handling; remind students seeds are not food; keep away from mouth |
| Water spills | Low | Use shallow trays under cups; keep paper towels nearby; direct supervision |
| Plastic wrap | Low | Use child-safe, soft-edged wrap; supervise during handling |

**Teacher Actions:**
- Pre-count seeds before distribution
- Explicitly state: "These seeds are for observation only—do not put them in your mouth"
- Place experiment cups on trays to contain any spills
- Designate a "seeds disposal" container if needed

### Picture Card & Sorting Activity

| Hazard | Risk Level | Mitigation |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Paper cuts from cards | Very Low | Use laminated cards or rounded-edge cards |
| Choking on small stickers | Low | Use larger stickers; supervise during craft portions |

### "My Pet's Care Guide" Craft Activity

| Hazard | Risk Level | Mitigation |

## 🧹 General Classroom Safety

### Before the Lesson

- [ ] Verify all materials are age-appropriate and non-toxic
- [ ] Check for student allergies (seeds, plants, craft materials)
- [ ] Prepare a clean workspace with easy cleanup access
- [ ] Ensure adequate adult supervision ratio (1 adult per 6-8 students for hands-on activities)

### During the Lesson

- [ ] Keep a bin of hand sanitizer/wipes accessible
- [ ] Remind students to keep materials on their workspace, not on the floor
- [ ] Circulate to ensure proper handling of all items
- [ ] Maintain clear traffic paths to prevent tripping

### After the Lesson

- [ ] Dispose of any seeds or organic materials properly
- [ ] Wash or sanitize all reusable materials
- [ ] Store sharp or small items out of student reach
- [ ] Check floor for any dropped small items (seeds, craft pieces)

---

## 🚨 Emergency Procedures

### If a Student Ingests a Seed or Small Object

1. **Remain calm** — do not alarm the student
2. **Assess** — determine if the item was a seed or non-toxic material
3. **Notify** — contact school nurse immediately
4. **Document** — record the incident and time
5. **Monitor** — watch for any adverse reactions while awaiting nurse

### If Water Is Spilled

1. **Immediate cleanup** — use paper towels or mop
2. **Move students** from wet area to prevent slipping
3. **Place absorbent material** on floor while cleaning

If sending home any materials (e.g., seed observation kits):

> "This week's science lesson includes a seed experiment. All materials are non-toxic and child-safe. Please remind your child that the seeds are for observation only and should not be consumed."

## Summary

This lesson uses low-risk, non-toxic materials appropriate for Grade 1 students. The primary safety focus is:
- **Choking prevention** with small seeds/objects
- **Hygiene** (hand washing before/after handling materials)
- **Supervision** during all hands-on activities

With standard classroom supervision practices, this lesson presents minimal safety concerns.
