# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Cross-Curricular Connections

## Overview
This document maps the Grade 1 Science "Living Things" unit to Mathematics, Language Arts, and Visual Arts standards and activities.

---

## 🔢 Mathematics Connections

### Counting & Cardinality
- **Activity:** "Living Things Count" - Students count pictures of living vs. non-living things and record numbers on a tens frame
- **Skill:** Counting objects accurately, understanding "more/less/fewer"
- **Assessment:** Tally chart of items found in classroom nature walk

### Measurement
- **Activity:** "How Tall Am I?" - Measure plant growth over time using non-standard units (paper clips, cubes)
- **Skill:** Comparing heights, using comparative language (taller, shorter, tallest)
- **Integration:** Seeds experiment from Lesson 4

### Data & Graphing
- **Activity:** Create a bar graph showing "Our Favorite Living Things" (pets, farm animals, wild animals, plants)
- **Skill:** Reading simple graphs, interpreting data
- **Assessment:** Students answer questions like "How many more students chose dogs than cats?"

### Geometry & Spatial Reasoning
- **Activity:** "Living Thing Patterns" - Create AB patterns using pictures of animals/plants
- **Skill:** Identifying and extending patterns
- **Extension:** Build 2D shape animals (circle for body, triangles for ears)

### Addition/Subtraction
- **Activity:** "Animal Math Stories" - Word problems about living things
  - *Example:* "3 rabbits ate 2 carrots. How many carrots were eaten?"
- **Skill:** Solving simple addition/subtraction problems within 10

## 📚 Language Arts Connections

### Reading - Informational Text
- **Activity:** Read age-appropriate books about animals/plants:
  - *"The Very Hungry Caterpillar"* (Eric Carle)
  - *"From Seed to Plant"* (Gail Gibbons)
  - *"Animal Lives"* series (simple non-fiction)
- **Skill:** Identifying main idea, finding facts, text features (photos, captions)

### Writing - Informative/Explanatory
- **Activity:** "All About My Pet/Plant" - Students write 3-5 sentences describing their chosen living thing
- **Skill:** Using factual information, organizing writing with a title and details
- **Integration:** Connects to "My Pet's Care Guide" from Lesson 4

### Writing - Narrative
- **Activity:** "A Day in the Life of..." - Short story from perspective of an animal or plant
- **Skill:** Creative expression, sequence of events (first, then, next, finally)

### Speaking & Listening
- **Activity:** "Show and Tell" - Students bring/talk about a living thing (real or photo)
- **Skill:** Verbal communication, asking/answering questions, listening actively
- **Rubric:** Eye contact, clear speaking, relevant facts

### Vocabulary Development
- **Key Science Words:** living, non-living, grow, change, needs, food, water, air, shelter, habitat
- **Activity:** Word wall with pictures, sorting words by category
- **Assessment:** Students use words in complete sentences

### Phonics/Word Work
- **Activity:** "Living Things Word Search" - Find names of animals/plants
- **Skill:** Letter recognition, spelling high-frequency words
- **Extension:** Sort words by beginning sounds, rhyming words (cat, bat, hat)

## 🎨 Visual Arts Connections

### Drawing & Illustration
- **Activity:** "My Pet's Care Guide" (from Lesson 4)
- **Skill:** Drawing recognizable subjects, adding details
- **Focus:** Showing understanding through visual representation

### Nature Art
- **Activity:** "Leaf Rubbings" - Use leaves to create texture art
- **Activity:** "Animal Sketches" - Observational drawing of photos or real specimens
- **Skill:** Observational skills, understanding texture and form

### Collage & Mixed Media
- **Activity:** "Living Things Collage" - Students cut magazines/newspapers to create collages of living things and their habitats
- **Skill:** Cutting, gluing, composition

### Color Theory
- **Activity:** "Seasons of a Tree" - Show how trees change through seasons using different color palettes
- **Skill:** Recognizing colors, understanding seasonal changes

### 3D Art
- **Activity:** "Egg Carton Creatures" - Create animals from recycled materials
- **Skill:** Three-dimensional construction, creativity
- **Integration:** Discuss what makes these "living" vs. "non-living"

### Artist Studies
- **Activity:** Look at nature art by Georgia O'Keeffe (flower close-ups), Andy Goldsworthy (natural sculptures)
- **Skill:** Art appreciation, connecting art to science topics

## 📋 Cross-Curricular Lesson Ideas

### Integrated Theme: "Our Classroom Garden"
| Subject | Activity |
|---------|----------|
| **Science** | Plant seeds, observe growth, document changes |
| **Math** | Measure plants, graph growth over time |
| **Language Arts** | Write plant journal entries, read seed books |
| **Visual Arts** | Draw plant life cycle, create plant collage |

### Integrated Theme: "Animal Kingdom"
| Subject | Activity |
|---------|----------|
| **Science** | Sort animals by characteristics (fish/bird/mammal) |
| **Math** | Count animal legs, create tally charts |
| **Language Arts** | Research simple animal facts, write animal riddles |
| **Visual Arts** | Create animal masks, paint animal habitats |

## ✅ Assessment Cross-Curricular

| Subject | Assessment |
|---------|------------|
| **Math** | Graph interpretation quiz, measurement recording sheet |
| **Language Arts** | Writing rubric for "All About..." piece, participation in discussions |
| **Visual Arts** | Artwork portfolio, self-assessment "I am proud of..." reflection |

---

## 📝 Teacher Notes

- **Integration Tip:** Choose 1-2 cross-curricular activities per week to avoid overwhelming students
- **Differentiation:** Adjust complexity based on student readiness (e.g., simpler graphs, sentence starters for writing)
- **Parent Involvement:** Send home "Living Things Hunt" worksheet for family activity
- **Standards Alignment:** All activities support Grade 1 curriculum standards in each subject area
