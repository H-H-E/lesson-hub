# Grade 1 Science: Living Things - Learning Objectives

## SMART Objectives

1. **Specific:** Students will identify and name at least 5 living things (animals and plants) from pictures or real specimens.
   - **Measurable:** Correctly identifies 5+ living things with 80% accuracy
   - **Achievable:** Grade 1 students can recognize common animals and plants
   - **Relevant:** Builds foundation for understanding the natural world
   - **Time-bound:** Achieved by the end of Week 2

2. **Specific:** Students will describe three ways living things grow and change over time.
   - **Measurable:** Names 3 growth changes (e.g., getting bigger, losing baby teeth, caterpillars becoming butterflies)
   - **Achievable:** Observable changes are accessible to young learners
   - **Relevant:** Connects to students' own growth experiences
   - **Achieved by:** End of Week 3

3. **Specific:** Students will distinguish between living and non-living things using three key characteristics (movement, food/air needs, growth).
   - **Measurable:** Correctly categorizes 8/10 items as living or non-living
   - **Achievable:** Characteristics are concrete and observable
   - **Relevant:** Helps students understand their environment
   - **Achieved by:** End of Week 4

4. **Specific:** Students will explain why living things need food, water, and air to survive.
   - **Measurable:** States at least 2 reasons why living things need these basic needs
   - **Achievable:** Basic needs are familiar concepts to 6-7 year olds
   - **Relevant:** Relates to students' own daily needs
   - **Achieved by:** End of Week 5
