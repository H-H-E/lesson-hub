# Grade 1 Science - Living Things
## Systems Literacy Connection

## 🌍 Systems Literacy Connection

> *"You are not a lone creature — you are a web of relationships."*

**Key Metaphor:** Living Things = A Garden

Just like a garden has flowers, soil, sun, water, and bees all working together — living things don't exist alone. Animals need plants (for food and shelter). Plants need animals (for pollination). We need both! Every living thing is connected to many others.

---

## 🔗 Systems Thinking Connection

### What is a System?
A system is when parts work together — like a team! A garden is a system. A forest is a system. Your body is a system with parts that all help each other.

### How Living Things Connect

**Food Webs (Simplified for Grade 1):**
- Plants grow from soil and sun
- Animals eat plants
- Animals eat other animals
- Dead plants and animals become soil again
- The cycle keeps going!

**Relationships:**
- Bees help flowers make seeds (pollination)
- Worms make soil healthy for plants
- Animals spread seeds when they eat fruit
- People take care of pets and gardens

### Big Idea for Young Learners
**"Everything is connected!"**

When we learn about living things, we're not just learning names — we're learning how we all belong to the same big family of life on Earth.

## 🔄 Circle of Life (Simple Systems Model)

```
    ☀️ Sun
     ↓
  🌱 Plants ← 🐛 Animals
     ↓        ↘
    🐛      💩 Decomposers
     ↓          ↓
    🌱 (soil helps plants grow again!)
```

This simple circle shows:
- Living things need each other
- Nothing is wasted
- The Earth keeps providing

## 🎯 Teacher Guidance

### Introducing Systems Thinking to Grade 1

1. **Start with what they know:** Family, friends, classmates — people who help us and that we help
2. **Use the garden metaphor:** It's concrete, observable, and relatable
3. **Ask connection questions:**
   - "Who else needs this plant/animal?"
   - "What would happen if there were no more bees?"
   - "How do you help other people/animals?"

### Cross-Module Connections
This systems thinking foundation connects to:
- **Matter** (Module 2): Living things are made of matter too!
- **Energy** (future modules): Living things need energy from the sun
- **Earth Systems** (future modules): All living things share Earth

## 📚 Reflection Questions

### For Student Journals
1. What is one living thing you saw today? Who else is connected to it?
2. How do you help other people or animals?
3. If there were no more trees, what would happen to the birds?

### Class Discussion Prompts
- "Are humans part of a system? How?"
- "Can a living thing survive all by itself? Why or why not?"
- "What's your favorite connection between living things?"
