# Grade 1 Science: Living Things — Parent Guide

---

## 🎯 What Your Child Is Learning

In this unit, your first-grader is exploring the fascinating world of **Living Things**. They are learning to:

- **Identify living vs. non-living things** — Understanding what makes something "alive"
- **Recognize that living things grow and change** — Like caterpillars becoming butterflies or puppies becoming dogs
- **Discover what living things need to survive** — Food, water, air, and shelter

---

## 📚 Vocabulary Words

Here are the key words your child is learning. Try using them at home!

| Word | Simple Definition |
|------|-------------------|
| **Living** | Something that is alive — it moves, grows, and needs food, water, and air |
| **Non-living** | Something that was never alive (like a rock or toy) |
| **Grow** | To get bigger over time |
| **Change** | To become different |
| **Food** | What living things eat for energy |
| **Water** | What living things drink to stay healthy |
| **Air** | The invisible gas we breathe |
| **Shelter** | A home or place that protects living things |
| **Seed** | A tiny package that can grow into a new plant |
| **Needs** | Things a living thing must have to survive |

## 💬 Discussion Questions

Use these questions to spark conversations with your child:

### At the Dinner Table
- "Is a stuffed animal alive? Why or why not?"
- "What do our pet [dog/cat/fish] need to stay healthy?"
- "Can you think of something living that doesn't have legs?"

### On a Walk or Outdoor Play
- "Is that a living thing or non-living thing? How do you know?"
- "What is growing or changing in our yard/garden?"
- "What does that tree need to live?"

### Before Bed
- "What did you learn about living things today?"
- "What would happen if a plant didn't have water?"
- "How have you grown since you were a baby?"

## 🏠 Home Activities

### Easy Activities (5-10 minutes)

**Living vs. Non-Living Sort**
Gather 10 items from around your home (toy, rock, plant, book, spoon, pet, etc.). Ask your child to sort them into two groups: living and non-living. Ask them to explain why they made each choice!

**Plant Watch**
Place a dry bean seed in a clear cup with a wet paper towel. Watch it over several days. Draw or describe what changes your child notices each day.

**Body Growth Chart**
Measure your child's height and mark it on a wall or door frame. Compare it to baby photos. Talk about how they have grown and changed!

### Fun Projects (20-30 minutes)

**My Pet Care Guide**
Have your child create a small booklet (folded paper) for a pet or animal they love. They can draw:
- Page 1: The animal
- Page 2: What it eats
- Page 3: Where it lives
- Page 4: What would happen without water or air

**Nature Collection Walk**
Take a walk around your neighborhood or a park. Have your child collect (or photograph) 5 living things and 5 non-living things. Talk about how they know each one belongs in its group!

**Grow a Seed**
Plant a simple seed (beans, grass, or sunflower) in a small pot. Help your child care for it and record its growth in a journal with drawings each week.

## 🔑 Quick Parent Tips

1. **Use the vocabulary** — Even simple words like "living," "grow," and "needs" help reinforce classroom learning.

2. **Ask open-ended questions** — "Why do you think...?" gets kids thinking more than yes/no questions.

3. **Connect to real life** — Cooking (plants grow), pets (need food/water), and their own bodies (they're growing!) are all opportunities.

4. **Make it fun, not formal** — Games and conversations work better than worksheets at this age!

## 📖 Want to Learn More?

- Watch simple nature videos about how animals grow
- Visit a local park or zoo and look for living things together
