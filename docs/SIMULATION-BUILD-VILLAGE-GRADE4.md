# Grade 4 Social Studies: Early Societies - Interactive Simulation

## "Build Your Own Village" Simulation

### Teacher Overview

**Time Required:** 2-3 class periods (90-120 minutes)  
**Group Size:** 3-4 students per group  
**Grade Level:** Grade 4

---

## Learning Goals

- Understand how geography affects where people settle
- Identify necessary components of a successful community
- Compare ancient societies with modern communities
- Practice collaboration and decision-making skills

---

## Materials Per Group

- Large poster board or butcher paper
- Markers, crayons, colored pencils
- Reference cards (provided below)
- "Resource Dice" (or regular dice)
- Decision scenario cards

---

## Simulation Steps

### Day 1: Setting the Stage (30 min)

**Step 1: Choose Your Location**
Each group receives a geography card:

| Card | Geography | Pros | Cons |
|------|-----------|------|------|
| A | Near a river | Water for farming, transport | Flood risk |
| B | Near the coast | Fishing, trade | Storms |
| C | In a valley | Good soil, shelter | Limited space |
| D | On a mountain | Defense, minerals | Hard to farm |

**Step 2: Roll for Resources**
Roll the dice to determine available resources:
- 1-2: Limited resources (harder challenge)
- 3-4: Moderate resources
- 5-6: Abundant resources

---

### Day 2: Building the Village (45 min)

**Required Buildings** (must include all 5):
1. 🏠 Housing - Where people live
2. 💧 Water system - Getting clean water
3. 🌾 Food storage - Storing and growing food
4. 🛡️ Defense - Protecting the village
5. ⛪ Community space - For gathering/religious use

**Bonus Buildings** (choose 2):
- Market/Trading post
- School
- Hospital/Healer hut
- Workshop/Artisan area
- Monument/Temple

**Design Requirements:**
- Draw each building
- Label what materials are used
- Explain WHY you placed buildings where you did

---

### Day 3: Challenges & Adaptations (30 min)

Groups draw scenario cards and must adapt:

| Scenario | Challenge | Solution |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Drought! Crops are dying | Build irrigation / find new food source |
| 2 | Enemy attack! | Build stronger walls / form alliance |
| 3 | Flood! Village damaged | Move buildings higher / build dams |
| 4 | New traders arrive | Build market / trade goods |
| 5 | Sickness spreads | Build healer hut / improve sanitation |
| 6 | Population grows! | Expand housing / build new areas |

---

## Discussion Questions

1. What was the hardest decision your group made? Why?
2. How did geography shape your village?
3. What would you do differently if you started over?
4. How is your ancient village similar to our community today?
5. What ancient societies can you think of that faced similar challenges?

---

## Assessment Rubric

| Criteria | Excellent (3) | Good (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|--------------|----------|----------------|
| Geography choice | Thoughtful reasoning | Basic reasoning | Little reasoning |
| Required buildings | All 5 complete | 3-4 complete | 1-2 complete |
| Design detail | Very detailed | Some detail | Minimal detail |
| Collaboration | Everyone contributed | Most contributed | Uneven participation |
| Problem-solving | Creative solutions | Standard solutions | Struggled to adapt |

---

## Differentiation

**For struggling learners:**
- Provide a template with building outlines
- Partner with a stronger student
- Reduce to 3 required buildings

**For advanced learners:**
- Add economy simulation (trade goods)
- Research real ancient societies and compare
- Create a "history book" page about their village

---

## Cross-Curricular Connections

- **ELA:** Write a "day in the life" story from a villager's perspective
- **Math:** Calculate population growth, resource math
- **Art:** Create village artifacts, pottery designs
- **Science:** Study materials used for buildings, climate impact
