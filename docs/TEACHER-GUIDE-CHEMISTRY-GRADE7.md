# Grade 7 Science: Pure Substances and Mixtures - Teacher Guide

## Unit Overview

**Duration:** 3 weeks (15 lessons)  
**Grade Level:** 7  
**Curriculum Area:** Science - Chemistry

### BC Curriculum Big Ideas
- Elements consist of one type of atom
- Compounds consist of atoms chemically combined
- Mixtures are physically combined substances

---

## Lesson 1: Matter Around Us (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Define matter, pure substance, and mixture
- Identify examples in everyday life

### Materials
- Samples of matter (water, salt, sugar, sand, oil)
- Magnifying glasses
- Classification chart

### Hook
Show students a tray of items:
- Salt, water, sand, salad, brass (copper + zinc)

**Think-Pair-Share:** "How would you sort these?"

### Direct Instruction (15 min)

**Key Vocabulary:**
- **Matter:** Anything that has mass and takes up space
- **Pure Substance:** Made of only one type (element or compound)
- **Mixture:** Two or more substances combined but not chemically bonded

**Classification:**
```
Matter
├── Pure Substances
│   ├── Elements (one type of atom)
│   │   Examples: Gold, Oxygen, Carbon
│   └── Compounds (atoms chemically bonded)
│       Examples: Water (H2O), Salt (NaCl)
└── Mixtures
    ├── Homogeneous (evenly mixed)
    │   Examples: Salt water, Air
    └── Heterogeneous (unevenly mixed)
        Examples: Salad, Sand and water
```

### Activity (20 min)
Students sort provided samples into categories and justify their reasoning.

## Lesson 2: Elements and the Periodic Table (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Understand what elements are
- Become familiar with the periodic table
- Identify some common elements

### Activities

**1. Element Charades**
- Groups act out an element
- Class guesses (Oxygen, Carbon, Gold, etc.)

**2. Periodic Table Exploration**
- Groups examine the periodic table
- Find: metals, non-metals, noble gases
- Identify pattern: atomic number

**3. Element Scavenger Hunt**
- Find elements in the classroom
- Iron in scissors? Carbon in pencil lead?

---

## Lesson 3: Compounds - When Atoms Combine (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Understand how compounds form
- Know that compounds have different properties than elements

### Demonstration
**Making a compound:**
- Show: Iron filings + Sulfur powder (gray + yellow = gray-yellow mix)
- Heat strongly (demonstrate or show video)
- Result: Iron sulfide (black compound!)

**Key Point:** Compounds have NEW properties!

### Activity
Students build molecules using:
- Ball-and-stick models
- Or online simulation (PhET)

### Compounds to Know

| Compound | Formula | Elements | Properties |
|----------|---------|----------|------------|
| Water | H2O | Hydrogen + Oxygen | Liquid, clear |
| Salt | NaCl | Sodium + Chloride | White, salty |
| Carbon dioxide | CO2 | Carbon + Oxygen | Gas |
| Sugar | C6H12O6 | C, H, O | Sweet, white |

## Lessons 4-5: Mixtures (2 x 60 min)

### Types of Mixtures

**1. Homogeneous (Solutions)**
- Salt water
- Air
- Brass
- Kool-Aid

**2. Heterogeneous**
- Sand in water
- Salad
- Rocky road ice cream
- Italian dressing

### Lab: Making Mixtures (Lesson 4)

Students create and observe:
- Mixture A: Sand + Water
- Mixture B: Salt + Water
- Mixture C: Oil + Water

Record observations over time.

### Filtration and Separation (Lesson 5)

**Key Question:** How can we separate mixtures?

**Methods:**
- Filtration (filter paper)
- Evaporation
- Magnetism
- Decanting

**Lab Stations:**
1. Filter sand + water
2. Evaporate salt water
3. Separate iron filings + sand (magnet)
4. Separate oil + water (decant)

---

## Lesson 6: Concentration (60 min)

### Concept
- **Concentrated:** Lots of solute in solvent
- **Diluted:** Little solute in solvent

### Activity: Dilution Series

Students make solutions with different concentrations:
- 1 spoon sugar + 100ml water = concentration A
- 2 spoons + 100ml = concentration B
- 4 spoons + 100ml = concentration C

**Taste test (optional):** Compare!

## Lesson 7: Assessment and Review

### Option A: Mixture Lab Report
Students design an experiment to separate a mystery mixture

### Option B: Concept Map
Create visual showing matter → pure substances + mixtures

### Option C: Kahoot Quiz
Review game with team competition
