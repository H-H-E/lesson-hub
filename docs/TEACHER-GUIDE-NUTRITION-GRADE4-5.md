# Grade 4-5 Health: Nutrition and Healthy Eating - Teacher Guide

## Unit Overview

**Duration:** 2 weeks (10 lessons)  
**Grades:** 4-5  
**Curriculum Area:** Health Education

### BC Curriculum Connections
- Healthy eating influences physical, emotional, and social well-being
- Food choices affect health and environment

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:
1. Identify the six essential nutrient groups
2. Create balanced meals using MyPlate model
3. Analyze food labels for nutritional content
4. Understand how food choices affect the environment
5. Develop personal healthy eating goals

---

## Lesson 1: Nutrients - What Food Gives Us (60 min)

### Materials
- Nutrient chart posters
- Food cards (pictures of various foods)
- MyPlate diagram
- Student notebooks

### Direct Instruction Content

**The Six Nutrient Groups:**

| Nutrient | What It Does | Food Sources |
|----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Carbohydrates** | Energy for our bodies | Bread, rice, pasta, fruit |
| **Proteins** | Build and repair muscles | Meat, eggs, beans, nuts |
| **Fats** | Energy storage, protect organs | Butter, oil, nuts, avocado |
| **Vitamins** | Keep us healthy | Fruits, vegetables |
| **Minerals** | Strong bones, blood | Dairy, leafy greens, meat |
| **Water** | Keeps us alive! | Water, fruits, vegetables |

### Activity: Nutrient Detective
Students sort food cards into nutrient groups.

---

## Lesson 2: MyPlate - Build a Balanced Meal (60 min)

### Review
Quick quiz on nutrients

### Introducing MyPlate

**The Plate Sections:**
- Fruits (20%)
- Vegetables (30%)
- Grains (30%)
- Protein (20%)
- Dairy (circle on side)

### Activity: Create a Meal

Students plan a breakfast, lunch, or dinner:
- Must include at least 3 food groups
- Draw the plate
- List foods and nutrients included

## Lessons 3-4: Reading Food Labels (2 x 60 min)

### Why Read Labels?

**Key Things to Look For:**
- Serving size
- Calories
- Sodium (salt)
- Sugar
- Fiber
- Ingredients list (first 3 are most important)

### Activity: Label Scavenger Hunt

Students bring in food packages (empty/clean):
- Find the nutrition facts
- Compare two similar products
- Which is healthier? Why?

### Comparison Chart

| Product | Calories | Sugar | Sodium | Fiber |

## Lesson 5: Sugar Shock (60 min)

### Hook
"How much sugar do you think is in this soda?"

### Demonstration
Show sugar cubes representing sugar in drinks:
- Soda can (10 cubes = 39g)
- Sports drink (7 cubes = 21g)
- Juice box (5 cubes = 16g)
- Water (0 cubes)

### Discussion
- Where else is sugar hiding?
- What does too much sugar do to our bodies?

### Activity
Students create a "Sugar Map" of their typical day's food and estimate sugar content.

---

## Lesson 6: Hydration Station (60 min)

### Facts About Water
- Our bodies are 60% water
- We need 6-8 glasses per day
- Water helps:
  - Digestion
  - Temperature control
  - Brain function
  - Moving nutrients

### Experiment
Students track water intake for one day using a water bottle with marks.

### Infographic Project
Create a poster: "Why Water is Awesome!"

---

## Lesson 7: Food and the Environment (60 min)

### Discussion
"How does food get to our table?"

### Topics
- Food miles (how far food travels)
- Local vs. imported
- Seasonal eating
- Food waste

### Activity: Food Miles Calculation
Students trace origins of their lunch:
- Where did each item come from?
- How many kilometers did it travel?

### Environmental Food Choices
- Eat more plants
- Less meat = better for planet
- Buy local when possible
- Reduce food waste

---

## Lessons 8-9: Personal Healthy Eating Goals (2 x 60 min)

### Reflection
Students complete a "My Food Diary" for 3 days

### Goal Setting Workshop

**SMART Goals for Eating:**
- **S**pecific: Eat vegetables at dinner
- **M**easurable: Eat 2 servings of vegetables daily
- **A**chievable: Start with 1 serving
- **R**elevant: I want more energy
- **T**ime-bound: For 2 weeks

### My Healthy Eating Plan
Students create their personal plan:
- 2 things to keep doing
- 1 thing to start doing
- 1 thing to eat less of

---

## Lesson 10: Healthy Eating Fair (60 min)

### Student-Led Celebration

Each student creates a station:
- Display MyPlate meal
- Nutrition fact poster
- Healthy recipe card
- Food label comparison

### Peer Learning
Students visit each other's stations
