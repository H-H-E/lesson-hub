# Lesson Plan: Living Things (Grade 1, Module 1)

## Overview
- **Grade:** 1
- **Topic:** Living vs Non-Living
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Materials:** Sorting cards, simulation, worksheets

---

## Learning Objectives

By end of lesson, students will be able to:
1. Sort objects into living/non-living categories (90% accuracy)
2. Explain what makes something alive (with prompting)
3. Use words: living, non-living, needs

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Part 1: Hook (5 min)

**Teacher says:**
"Today we're going to be scientists! Look at these pictures. Which ones are alive?"

**Show:**
- Dog, tree, rock, car, person, sun, fish, cloud

**Students discuss:**
- What does "alive" mean?

---

### Part 2: Exploration (15 min)

**Activity:** Interactive Simulation

Students go to computers/tablets:
1. Open simulation link
2. Drag items to "Living" or "Non-Living" bins
3. Get immediate feedback
4. Try to get 100%

**While students work:**
- Circulate and ask: "How do you know?"
- Note misconceptions

---

### Part 3: Discovery (10 min)

**Class discussion:**

Q: What did you notice about living things?
A: They move, grow, need food/water/air

Q: What about non-living?
A: They don't do those things

**Introduce vocabulary:**
- Living, non-living, needs

---

### Part 4: Create (10 min)

**Activity:** Sorting Cards

Students work in pairs:
1. Get set of picture cards
2. Sort onto chart
3. Check with partner
4. Add one more example

---

### Part 5: Check (5 min)

**Exit Ticket:**
Draw one living thing and one non-living thing.
Label each.

---

## Differentiation

### Level 1 (Support)
- Pre-sorted examples to reference
- Teacher works with small group
- Simplified vocabulary

### Level 2 (Guided)
- Partner work
- Sentence stems

### Level 3 (Extension)
- Add third category: "Was never living"
- Create own examples

---

## Assessment

| Type | What | When |
|------|------|------|
| Observation | Participation, understanding | During exploration |
| Discussion | Vocabulary use | During discovery |
| Exit ticket | Sorting accuracy | End |

---

## First Peoples Integration

**Connection:**
- Secwepemc classification of plants/animals
- Traditional knowledge of local species
- Language: Secwepemc words for animals

---

## Teacher Notes

- Common misconception: Sun is living (needs light!)
- Cars move but aren't alive
- Some things are tricky: seeds, dormant animals
- Make connections to students' lives
