## Lesson Modules (Code-First Inquiry)

### Module 1: "Living Things in Our World" 🌿🐌

**Duration:** 45 minutes

#### Standards Alignment
- **Science**: Living things have features and behaviours that help them survive
- **Science**: Classification of living/non-living
- **ADST**: Generate ideas from experiences
- **Math**: Sorting and classifying

#### Learning Objectives (SMART)

| # | Objective | Students will be able to... |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Identify | Identify living vs non-living things with 90% accuracy |
| 2 | Describe | Describe one way animals move |
| 3 | Explain | Explain why different animals move differently |

#### Scaffolding Plan

**LEVEL 1 - HIGHEST SUPPORT**
- Teacher shows completed sorting chart
- Students match labels to pictures
- Guided: "Point to the living thing"
- Visual: Color-coded cards (green=living, gray=non-living)

**LEVEL 2 - GUIDED SUPPORT**
- Students sort with partner
- Partially filled chart to complete
- Sentence stems: "A _____ is living because it _____"

**LEVEL 3 - INDEPENDENT**
- Open sorting challenge
- Create own categories
- Extension: Add new items to sort

#### Assessment

**Formative:**
- [x] Exit ticket: Draw one living and one non-living thing
- [x] Observation checklist during sorting activity
- [x] Discussion: "How do you know?"

**Summative:**
- Project: Create "Living Things in Our Community" poster

### Module 2: "Matter Matters" 💧🪨

**Duration:** 45 minutes

#### Standards Alignment
- **Science**: Matter is useful because of its properties
- **Science**: Solids keep shape; liquids and gases flow
- **Math**: Measurement (comparing)

#### Learning Objectives (SMART)

| # | Objective | Students will be able to... |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Identify | Name the 3 states of matter with examples |
| 2 | Observe | Describe what happens when ice melts |
| 3 | Predict | Predict what will happen when heat is added |

#### Scaffolding Plan

**LEVEL 1 - HIGHEST SUPPORT**
- Worksheet with labeled states of matter
- Picture matching: ice → water (arrow) water
- Teacher-led: "Watch what happens when I add heat"

**LEVEL 2 - GUIDED SUPPORT**
- Sort objects into states with partner
- Fill in blank: "_____ keeps its shape"
- Prediction prompt: "I think the ice will _____"

**LEVEL 3 - INDEPENDENT**
- Design experiment: "Make water move"
- Extension: Create own state change demonstration

#### Assessment

**Formative:**
- [x] Exit ticket: Draw matter in 3 states
- [x] Prediction share-out
- [x] Observation of interaction with simulation

**Summative:**
- Project: "Matter in Our Classroom" collection

---

### Module 3: "Light & Shadow Play" ☀️🌑

**Duration:** 45 minutes

#### Standards Alignment
- **Science**: Light and sound can be produced and properties changed
- **Science**: Objects made visible by light or reflection
- **Science**: Shadows created by light being blocked

#### Learning Objectives (SMART)

| # | Objective | Students will be able to... |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Create | Create a shadow using a light source |
| 2 | Observe | Describe how shadows change throughout the day |
| 3 | Explain | Explain why shadows form |

#### Scaffolding Plan

**LEVEL 1 - HIGHEST SUPPORT**
- Teacher demonstrates with flashlight
- Trace shadow outline on paper
- Guided questions: "Where is the shadow?"

**LEVEL 2 - GUIDED SUPPORT**
- Student pairs explore with flashlights
- Shadow size prediction chart
- Sentence stem: "The shadow is _____ because _____"

**LEVEL 3 - INDEPENDENT**
- Create shadow art
- Explore: How to make shadow disappear?
- Extension: Make shadow tell a story

#### Assessment

**Formative:**
- [x] Exit ticket: Draw your shadow
- [x] Observation during exploration
- [x] Discussion: "Why do shadows change?"

**Summative:**
- Project: Shadow Puppet Show (with family)

### Module 4: "Patterns in the Sky" 🌙⭐

**Duration:** 45 minutes

#### Standards Alignment
- **Science**: Observable patterns and cycles in the sky
- **First Peoples**: Shared knowledge of the sky
- **Math**: Patterns and cycles

#### Learning Objectives (SMART)

| # | Objective | Students will be able to... |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Observe | Describe the moon shape on different nights |
| 2 | Identify | Identify the sun as a light source |
| 3 | Connect | Connect sky patterns to daily life |

#### Scaffolding Plan

**LEVEL 1 - HIGHEST SUPPORT**
- Moon phase cards in order
- Teacher-led: "This is how the moon looks"
- Visual: Picture sequence of moon phases

**LEVEL 2 - GUIDED SUPPORT**
- Moon journal (draw moon each night)
- Partner discussion about observations
- Connect to story: "In many cultures, the moon..."

**LEVEL 3 - INDEPENDENT**
- Create moon phase model
- Extension: Research moon stories from different cultures

#### First Peoples Integration

- Secwepemc (Shuswap) moon stories
- Local Indigenous perspectives on seasonal cycles
- Connection to "seasonal rounds"

#### Assessment

**Formative:**
- [x] Moon journal check
- [x] Exit ticket: Draw the moon tonight
- [x] Class sharing circle

**Summative:**
- Project: Sky Watcher's Booklet

### Module 5: "Sound Explorations" 🔊🎵

**Duration:** 45 minutes

#### Standards Alignment
- **Science**: Properties of sound (pitch, volume)
- **Science**: Ways to make, record, transmit sound
- **ADST**: Use tools to extend capabilities

#### Learning Objectives (SMART)

| # | Objective | Students will be able to... |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Explore | Make sounds using different materials |
| 2 | Describe | Describe sounds as loud/quiet, high/low |
| 3 | Investigate | Find how to change a sound |

#### Scaffolding Plan

**LEVEL 1 - HIGHEST SUPPORT**
- Sound station with teacher-led exploration
- Matching: sound → source picture
- Guided: "Shake this - what do you hear?"

**LEVEL 2 - GUIDED SUPPORT**
- Sound hunt around classroom
- Sound graph (loud vs quiet)
- Partner: "Show me how to make a high sound"

**LEVEL 3 - INDEPENDENT**
- Create instrument
- Extension: Make sound pattern/music

#### Assessment

**Formative:**
- [x] Exit ticket: Draw your favorite sound maker
- [x] Observation during stations
- [x] Verbal sharing

**Summative:**
- Project: Classroom Band performance

### Module 6: "Sorting & Classifying" 🔢

**Duration:** 45 minutes

#### Standards Alignment
- **Science**: Classification of living/non-living
- **Math**: Sorting, classifying
- **ADST**: Choose criteria for sorting

#### Learning Objectives (SMART)

| # | Objective | Students will be able to... |
|---|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Sort | Sort objects by one attribute |
| 2 | Explain | Explain how they sorted |
| 3 | Create | Create own sorting rules |

#### Scaffolding Plan

**LEVEL 1 - HIGHEST SUPPORT**
- Pre-sorted examples to examine
- Teacher-led sort with think-aloud
- Categories given: "Sort by color"

**LEVEL 2 - GUIDED SUPPORT**
- Sort with partner, explain reasoning
- Two-attribute sorts
- Sentence stem: "I sorted by _____ because _____"

**LEVEL 3 - INDEPENDENT**
- Create sorting challenge for classmate
- Extension: Multi-attribute sorting game

#### Assessment

**Formative:**
- [x] Exit ticket: Sort these 3 items
- [x] Observation during activity
- [x] Partner explanation

**Summative:**
- Project: Create "Sorting Museum" display
