# Grade 7 Health: Mental Health and Wellness - Teacher Guide

## Unit Overview

**Duration:** 4 weeks (20 lessons)  
**Grade:** 7  
**Curriculum Area:** Health Education

### BC Curriculum Connections
- Mental health literacy and stigma reduction
- Strategies for maintaining mental health
- Understanding emotions and stress management

---

## Week 1: Understanding Mental Health

### Lesson 1: What is Mental Health? (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Define mental health
- Understand mental health vs. mental illness
- Reduce stigma

### Hook (10 min)
Write on board: "Mental health is..."

Students brainstorm in pairs. Share with class.

### Direct Instruction (15 min)

**Key Messages:**
- Mental health is more than the absence of mental illness
- It's about overall wellbeing
- Everyone has mental health - it changes over time
- It's normal to have ups and downs

**Facts:**
- 1 in 5 Canadians experience mental health concerns
- It's okay to ask for help
- Recovery is possible

### Activity: Mental Health Continuum (20 min)

Students place themselves on a line from "Thriving" to "Struggling" for different areas:
- Physical health
- Emotional health
- Social health
- Academic stress

Discuss: "What helps us move toward thriving?"

### Reflection (10 min)
Write in journals: "One thing I learned about mental health today..."

---

### Lesson 2: Emotions and Feelings (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Identify and name emotions
- Understand that all emotions are valid

### Warm-Up: Emotion Charades

Students act out emotions, others guess:
- Happy, Sad, Angry, Scared
- Anxious, Excited, Embarrassed, Proud

### Discussion

**Key Question:** "Are some emotions bad?"

**Teach:** All emotions are normal and valid. It's what we do with them that matters.

### The Emotion Iceberg

Draw an iceberg:
- Top (visible): Behavior, what we show
- Underwater (hidden): Feelings, thoughts, needs

**Discussion:** Sometimes we can't see what someone is really feeling.

### Coping Strategies Introduction

Quick list of healthy coping strategies:
- Talk to someone
- Deep breathing
- Exercise
- Journaling
- Art/Music
- Sleep

## Week 2: Stress Management

### Lesson 3: Understanding Stress (60 min)

### Learning Goals
- Identify sources of stress
- Understand good stress vs. bad stress

### Hook: Stress Survey

Students check off stressors they experience:
- [ ] School tests
- [ ] Friendships
- [ ] Family issues
- [ ] Sports/activities
- [ ] Social media
- [ ] Making decisions

### Types of Stress

**Good Stress (Eustress):**
- New experiences
- Exciting challenges
- Helps us grow

**Bad Stress (Distress):**
- Too much pressure
- Feeling overwhelmed
- Lasts too long

### Stress Response

**Fight, Flight, Freeze:**
- Heart races
- Breathing faster
- Muscles tense
- Butterflies in stomach

**This is normal!** Our body is trying to protect us.

---

### Lesson 4: Coping Strategies (60 min)

### Deep Breathing Practice

**4-7-8 Breathing:**
- Breathe in for 4
- Hold for 7
- Breathe out for 8
- Repeat 3 times

### Other Strategies

**Physical:**
- Exercise/walk
- Stretch
- Sleep well

**Emotional:**
- Journal feelings
- Talk to someone
- Creative expression

**Social:**
- Spend time with friends
- Ask for help
- Set boundaries

### Personal Coping Plan

Students create their own plan:
- My top 3 stressors
- My top 3 coping strategies
- Who I can talk to

## Week 3: Building Resilience

### Lesson 5: What is Resilience? (60 min)

### Definition
The ability to bounce back from challenges

### Discussion
Think of a time you faced a challenge. How did you get through it?

### Resilience Factors
- Supportive relationships
- Problem-solving skills
- Positive thinking
- Self-care
- Hope for the future

### Growth Mindset
"I can't do this... yet"
"This is hard... but I'm learning"

### Activity: Strengths Discovery

Students identify their personal strengths and how they can use them during hard times.

---

### Lesson 6: Helping Others (60 min)

### Discussion
How can we support friends who are struggling?

### Warning Signs
- Withdrawal from friends
- Changes in behavior
- Giving away possessions
- Talking about death
- Significant mood changes

**If you're worried about a friend:**
1. Listen without judging
2. Tell a trusted adult
3. Don't keep secrets if someone's safety is at risk

### Resource List

Create a class resource list:
- School counselor
- Kids Help Phone: 1-800-668-6868
- Family doctor
- Trusted adults

## Week 4: Self-Care and Action

### Lesson 7: Self-Care Inventory (60 min)

### What is Self-Care?
Activities that help us maintain mental health

### Categories
- Physical: Sleep, food, exercise
- Emotional: Feelings, coping
- Social: Friends, family
- Mental: Learning, hobbies
- Spiritual: Purpose, meaning

### Self-Care Wheel

Students create a visual wheel showing all areas and rate: How am I doing in each?

### Goal Setting

Choose ONE area to improve:
- What will I do?
- When will I do it?
- How will I know it worked?
