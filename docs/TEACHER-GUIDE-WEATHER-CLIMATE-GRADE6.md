# Grade 6 Science: Weather and Climate - Teacher Guide

## Unit Overview

**Duration:** 3 weeks (15 lessons)  
**Grade Level:** Grade 6  
**Curriculum Area:** Science - Earth and Space Sciences

### Big Ideas (BC Curriculum)
- Weather and climate affect ecosystems and human activities
- Scientific understanding of weather patterns helps us make predictions
- Climate change has local and global impacts

---

## Lesson 1: What is Weather? (60 min)

### Learning Objectives
- Define weather and climate
- Identify factors that affect weather
- Use weather instruments

### Materials
- Thermometers
- Barometers (if available)
- Anemometers (homemade)
- Weather journals

### Lesson Outline

**Hook (5 min):** Show two photos - one sunny beach, one snowy mountain. "What's the weather like? How do you know?"

**Direct Instruction (15 min):**
- Weather vs. Climate definitions
- Factors: temperature, precipitation, wind, humidity, air pressure
- Introduce weather instruments

**Guided Practice (20 min):**
- Students measure classroom conditions
- Record in weather journals

**Independent Practice (15 min):**
- Create a weather dictionary with illustrations
- Homework: Track local weather for 1 week

---

## Lesson 2: The Water Cycle (60 min)

### Activity: "Water Cycle in a Bag"

**Materials:** Ziploc bags, water, tape, blue food coloring

**Procedure:**
1. Add water and 2 drops of blue food coloring to bag
2. Seal and tape to window
3. Observe over 3-5 days

**Students record:**
- Day 1: Where is the water?
- Day 2: What do you see on the sides?
- Day 3: Where does water collect now?

**Discussion:** Evaporation, condensation, precipitation, collection

---

## Lesson 3: Reading Weather Maps (60 min)

### Learning Objectives
- Read a simple weather map
- Identify weather symbols
- Make predictions based on map data

### Materials
- Printed weather maps (Environment Canada)
- Symbol legend chart
- Map analysis worksheet

### Key Symbols to Teach:
- ☀ Sunny
- ⛅ Partly cloudy
- ☁ Cloudy
- 🌧 Rain
- 🌨 Snow
- 💨 Wind direction
- H High pressure
- L Low pressure

### Practice Activity:
Students analyze a weather map and answer:
1. What is the temperature in Vancouver?
2. Where is it raining?
3. What direction is the wind blowing?

---

## Lesson 4: Clouds and Cloud Types (60 min)

### Learning Objectives
- Identify three main cloud types
- Connect cloud types to weather predictions

### Cloud Types Chart

| Type | Height | Shape | Weather Prediction |
|------|--------|-------|--------------------|
| Cirrus | High | Wispy, feathery | Fair weather |
| Cumulus | Middle | Puffy, round | Fair weather |
| Stratus | Low | Flat, layered | Rain possible |
| Cumulonimbus | All levels | Tall, dark | Thunderstorms |

### Activity: Cloud Observation
Students observe sky daily for 1 week, sketch clouds, and predict weather.

## Lessons 5-6: Climate Zones (2 x 60 min)

### Comparison Project

Students research one climate zone and create a poster:

**Zones:**
- Arctic/Tundra
- Temperate
- Tropical
- Desert

**Must include:**
- Location on world map
- Average temperature and precipitation
- Typical plants and animals
- What to pack for a visit!

---

## Lessons 7-10: Climate Change (4 x 60 min)

### Overview
This sensitive topic is approached through scientific evidence and student inquiry.

### Lesson 7: What is Climate Change?
- Greenhouse effect explained simply
- Carbon cycle review
- Evidence from ice cores, sea levels

### Lesson 8: Causes
- Human activities: burning fossil fuels, deforestation
- Natural factors: volcanic activity, solar cycles

### Lesson 9: Impacts
- Local: BC wildfires, pine beetle, sea level rise
- Global: Coral bleaching, species migration, extreme weather

### Lesson 10: Taking Action
- Individual actions
- School and community initiatives
- Student action plans

---

## Assessment Overview

### Formative Assessments
- Daily weather journal entries
- Cloud identification quizzes
- Exit tickets after each lesson

### Summative Assessment Options

**Option A: Weather Report (Group)**
- 5-minute weather presentation
- Use maps, symbols, predictions
- Present to another class or parents

**Option B: Climate Zone Travel Brochure**
- Creative project showcasing research
- Visual and written components

**Option C: Climate Action Project**
- Identify a local climate issue
- Propose solutions
- Present to class
