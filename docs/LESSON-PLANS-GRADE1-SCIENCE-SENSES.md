# Grade 1 Science: My Senses - Lesson Plan Collection

## Overview

**Grade:** 1  
**Duration:** 5 lessons (250 minutes total)  
**Curriculum Area:** Science - Biology/Health

### BC Curriculum Connections
- Living things have sensory requirements
- Our senses help us learn about the world

---

## Lesson 1: Our Five Senses (50 min)

### Learning Goals
- Identify the five senses
- Connect each sense to a body part

### Materials
- Senses poster
- Paper plates with labels (eyes, ears, nose, tongue, hands)
- Various objects for exploring (apple, bell, flower, cotton ball)

### Lesson Sequence

**Hook (5 min):** "How do you know it's raining? How do you know a cake smells good?"

**Direct Instruction (10 min):**
- Introduce: See with eyes, Hear with ears, Smell with nose, Taste with tongue, Touch with skin
- Body parts song

**Guided Practice (20 min):**
- Station rotation:
  - Station 1: Look - What do you see?
  - Station 2: Listen - What sounds can you hear?
  - Station 3: Touch - How does it feel?
  - Station 4: Smell - What do you smell?
  - Station 5: Taste - What does it taste like? (teacher-led)

**Wrap-up (10 min):** Fill in senses worksheet

---

## Lesson 2: Which Sense? (50 min)

### Learning Goals
- Identify which sense helps us with different tasks
- Predict which sense will be most useful

### Activity: Sensory Guessing Bags

**Materials:** Brown paper bags, various objects

**Procedure:**
1. Students reach in (without looking)
2. Guess the object using only touch
3. Discuss: Which sense did you use?

**Objects to include:**
- Apple (touch + smell)
- Bell (touch + sound)
- Cotton ball (touch)
- Orange (touch + smell)
- Feather (touch)

**Discussion:** When is each sense most important?

---

## Lesson 3: Eye Spy with My Little Eye (50 min)

### Learning Goals
- Explore how our eyes work
- Identify colors, shapes, and sizes

### Activities

**1. Eye Spy Game**
- "I spy something red..."
- Students find matching objects in classroom

**2. Looking Closely**
- Examine leaves, coins, or fabric with magnifying glass
- Draw what you see

**3. Blindfold Obstacle Course**
- Navigate simple obstacle course while blindfolded
- Discuss: Why is sight important for safety?

---

## Lesson 4: Sound Safari (50 min)

### Learning Goals
- Explore different sounds
- Understand that sounds can be loud, soft, high, low

### Activities

**1. Sound Walk**
- Walk quietly around school
- Stop when you hear something interesting
- Record sounds in journal

**2. Sound Sort**
- Categorize sounds:
  - Loud vs. Quiet
  - High vs. Low
  - Nature vs. Human-made

**3. Homemade Instruments**
- Create shakers from rice in cups
- Explore: What makes sounds louder?

---

## Lesson 5: Touch and Taste Exploration (50 min)

### Part A: Touch Stations

**Textures to explore:**
- Smooth (mirror, glass)
- Rough (sandpaper, bark)
- Soft (cotton ball, stuffed animal)
- Hard (rock, block)
- Cold (ice cube)
- Warm (water in warm cup)

**Discussion:** Why do we have different feelings on our skin?

### Part B: Taste Test (Teacher-Led Only)

**Safe tasting activities:**
- Sweet: sugar water
- Salty: salt water
- Sour: lemon juice (small sip)
- Bitter: unsweetened cocoa (optional)

**Important:** Check for allergies first! Never force students to taste.

---

## Assessment

### Daily Exit Tickets
- Draw one thing you learned today

### Unit Assessment
- Can students name all 5 senses?
- Can they match senses to body parts?
- Can they explain which sense to use for different situations?

---

## Differentiation

### For English Language Learners
- Use picture cards with words
- Focus on one sense at a time

### For Advanced Learners
- Research: How do animals use their senses differently?
- Create a "Senses Book" with illustrations

### For Students Needing Support
- Use real objects rather than pictures
- Partner with peer helper

---

## Family Connection

**Homework:** "Senses Scavenger Hunt"
Find at home:
- Something that smells good
- Something that makes a loud sound
- Something that feels soft
- Something you can taste
- Something you can see

Draw or photograph each item!
